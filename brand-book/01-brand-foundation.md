# Chapter 1: Brand Foundation

This chapter establishes the strategic foundation of the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} brand — the mission, vision, values, and positioning that everything else builds on. These are the decisions that should change least often and be understood by everyone in the organization.

## Mission

> **{{PROGRAM_NAME}} exists to help every player stay informed, in control, and connected to support — making responsible gambling feel like self-care, not a warning.**

The mission has three components:

1. **Informed**: Players understand how gambling works — odds, randomness, house edge, and risk
2. **In control**: Players have accessible, intuitive tools to set limits, track their play, and take breaks
3. **Connected to support**: When a player needs help, the path is clear, immediate, and judgment-free

### Customizing your mission

When adapting the mission for your organization, keep these principles:

- Lead with the player, not the company ("We help players..." not "We are committed to...")
- Include all three components: education, tools, and support access
- Avoid language that frames RG as an obligation ("We are required to...") — frame it as a genuine service
- Keep it to one sentence if possible; two at most

**Template**: *"{{PROGRAM_NAME}} helps [audience] [benefit] by [method]."*

## Vision

> **A world where responsible gambling tools are as intuitive and engaging as the games themselves.**

The vision addresses the core problem: RG tools today are typically an afterthought — clunky interfaces, buried menus, clinical language. The vision imagines a future where the same design craft applied to creating engaging games is applied to creating engaging player protection features.

### What the vision implies

- RG features deserve the same UX investment as revenue-generating features
- Player protection should be integrated into the experience, not bolted onto it
- The standard of success is engagement — do players actually use the tools?

## Values

### The four brand pillars

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} is built on four pillars. These are the non-negotiable principles that guide every decision — from copy on a deposit limit screen to the design of a casino floor poster.

---

### 1. Educational

**We help players understand how gambling actually works.**

Most players have misconceptions about gambling. They believe in "hot" machines, "due" numbers, and systems that can beat the house. These myths don't just cost money — they undermine a player's ability to make informed choices.

The Educational pillar means:
- Explaining concepts like odds, house edge, and randomness in plain language
- Correcting common myths without being condescending
- Providing information proactively — before a player is in trouble, not only after
- Making educational content interesting, not academic

**In practice**: A reality check notification doesn't just say "You've been playing for 2 hours." It says "You've been playing for 2 hours. Most sessions average 45 minutes. Would you like to set a session reminder for next time?"

---

### 2. Supportive

**We connect players to help without judgment.**

The single biggest barrier to seeking help for gambling problems is stigma. Players fear being labeled, excluded, or looked down on. The Supportive pillar directly counters this by making every interaction with the RG brand feel safe, confidential, and respectful.

The Supportive pillar means:
- Help information is always visible and accessible — not hidden in a submenu
- Language never shames, blames, or pathologizes (see [Chapter 4: Voice and Tone](04-voice-and-tone.md))
- Support resources are presented as a normal part of the gambling experience, not an emergency measure
- Confidentiality is explicitly stated, not assumed

**In practice**: A self-assessment tool is introduced as "Lots of people check in on their habits — it takes 2 minutes" rather than "Are you worried you might have a gambling problem?"

---

### 3. Approachable

**We communicate like a trusted friend, not a warning label.**

This is the pillar that most differentiates {{PROGRAM_NAME}} from traditional RG programs. Most RG content reads like it was written by a compliance department — because it was. {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content reads like it was written by someone who genuinely cares about the reader's experience.

The Approachable pillar means:
- Warm, conversational tone — never clinical or bureaucratic
- Modern design sensibility — clean, fresh, inviting (think wellness app, not government form)
- Content that players *want* to read, not content they're forced to acknowledge
- Humor where appropriate (myth-busting, educational content) — never about gambling harm itself

**In practice**: An onboarding screen says "Before you dive in, let's set up a few things to keep your experience fun" rather than "Pursuant to regulatory requirements, you must acknowledge the following responsible gambling information."

---

### 4. Empowering

**We give players tools and agency to stay in control.**

Empowerment means treating players as capable adults who can make their own decisions when given the right information and tools. The opposite of empowerment is paternalism — telling players what to do, restricting their choices without explanation, or framing RG tools as things imposed *on* them rather than offered *to* them.

The Empowering pillar means:
- Players choose their own limits (with guidance on what's typical)
- Setting a limit is framed as a smart choice, not a requirement for risky players
- The language of control ("your limits," "your choice," "your pace") runs through every touchpoint
- Taking a break is presented as a sign of strength, not an admission of weakness

**In practice**: A deposit limit confirmation says "You're in control — your weekly limit of $200 is now active. You can adjust this anytime in your settings" rather than "Your account has been restricted to a maximum deposit of $200 per week."

---

## Brand positioning

### Where {{PROGRAM_NAME}} sits

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} occupies a specific position in the player's experience:

```
Entertainment brand          {{PROGRAM_NAME}}           Crisis support
(casino, sportsbook)        (wellness layer)           (helplines, treatment)

"Come play!"            "Play with direction."      "We're here to help."

Excitement               Awareness & tools           Recovery & treatment
Revenue-focused          Player-focused              Health-focused
```

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} is **not** the entertainment brand (it doesn't promote gambling) and it's **not** a crisis service (it doesn't provide clinical treatment). It's the **wellness layer** between the two — the resource that helps players stay in the entertainment zone and know where to go if they need more support.

### Positioning relative to the operator's brand

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} should feel like a **peer** to the operator's entertainment brand, not a subordinate appendage:

| Dimension | Entertainment brand | {{PROGRAM_NAME}} |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Engage players with entertainment | Help players stay in control |
| Tone | Exciting, aspirational | Warm, grounding, modern |
| Visual weight | Primary brand presence | Equal but distinct presence |
| Placement | Prominent across all channels | Visible and accessible, never hidden |
| Design investment | High (revenue driver) | Equal (player protection driver) |

**Key principle**: If the RG content on your platform looks noticeably cheaper, older, or less polished than the commercial content, you have a brand equity problem. Players notice when something is an afterthought.

### Positioning relative to existing RG programs

| Program | Focus | Compass difference |
|---|---|---|
| GameSense (BCLC/MA) | On-site advisor model, myth-busting | Compass is a deployable brand system, not an advisor program |
| GambleAware (UK) | Public awareness, research funding | Compass provides operator-level implementation, not just public messaging |
| NCPG (US) | Policy advocacy, helpline, campaign toolkits | Compass is a complete brand system, not a campaign toolkit |
| Operator-built programs | Custom, proprietary, variable quality | Compass is standardized, open-source, evidence-based |

## Key audiences

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} content must work for multiple audiences. Each audience has different needs, but all share the same brand experience.

### Primary: All players (universal prevention)

The largest audience and the default voice. These are players who gamble recreationally and may never develop a problem. The goal is to help them stay informed and in control — normalizing RG as part of the gambling experience for everyone, not just people in trouble.

### Secondary audiences

| Audience | What they need from {{PROGRAM_NAME}} |
|---|---|
| **At-risk players** | Early recognition tools (self-assessment), easy limit-setting, non-judgmental language that doesn't assume a problem |
| **Help-seekers** | Clear, immediate path to support (helpline, chat, self-exclusion). No barriers, no friction. |
| **Friends and family** | Information about signs of gambling harm, guidance on how to start a conversation, resources for their own support |
| **Young adults (18-25)** | Age-appropriate messaging that respects their autonomy; emphasis on financial literacy and odds education |
| **Sports bettors** | Context-specific messaging (in-play betting risks, accumulator myths, cash-out psychology) |
| **Staff** | Training content, scripts for sensitive conversations, confidence to refer players to support |
| **Regulators** | Evidence of compliance, demonstration of program quality and reach |
