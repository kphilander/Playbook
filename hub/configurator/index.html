    Step 2 of 7

    Color Palette

    Not sure where to start? Tap a ready-made palette below — or set your own three colors.

    New to picking colors? Read this first

    You don’t need a design background. The quickest path: tap one of the ready-made palettes — each is professionally matched and already easy to read. Then, if you like, nudge a color to match your brand.

    Under the hood every template uses three roles: Primary (dark card and page backgrounds), Secondary (highlights, badges, interactive elements), and Accent (buttons and big stat numbers). All the lighter and darker shades are generated for you.

    The label next to each color tells you, in plain words, whether text will be easy to read on it — and what to change if it isn’t. Open the advanced section only if you want to hand-tune text, background, and status colors.

    Ready-made palettes Tap one to apply it everywhere. Each is matched to look good and stay easy to read — you can fine-tune afterward.
    Your brand accent

    Step 3 of 7

    Typography

    Choose heading and body fonts from Google Fonts, or upload your own.

    How fonts flow through the system

    Three font roles cover everything: Heading (titles, wordmark, stat numbers), Body (paragraphs and captions), and Monospace (odds, percentages, helpline numbers — figures align cleanly).

    An uploaded brand font is embedded directly into your brand-inject.css as a data URL, so it works anywhere without hosting font files. The type scale controls how large each text level renders across all templates.

    Upload a .woff2, .woff, .ttf, or .otf file. The font will be auto-selected as the heading font and added to all selectors below. Single-weight fonts use synthesized bold for headings.

    Step 4 of 7

    Voice & Culture

    How your content sounds — and how it adapts across cultures. Defaults match the Playbook base profile (Western, individualist, direct).

    What does the cultural profile change?

    The five dimensions below rewrite the actual copy across templates — watch the myth-buster and support cards on the right change as you switch them. Voice sets who’s talking, framing sets who benefits, humor and directness set the register, and comfort sets how openly gambling is discussed.

    The tone preferences and avoid-list keep Tier 1 content stigma-free: words like “problem gambler” never appear in entertainment-literacy content, only in support contexts.

    Preferred Language
    Term-by-term guidance for content creators — e.g. say “player”, not “gambler”.
    Clinical or stigmatizing terms that never appear in entertainment-literacy content.
    Per-country overrides of the five dimensions above, applied at build time when rendering localized collateral. Each new country starts from your brand-wide profile.

    Step 5 of 7

    Messaging & Content

    Pillars, taglines, calls to action, and the default copy on your templates.

    Where does this copy show up?

    Pillars are the two promises your content makes (Playbook’s are “Open” and “Social”) — they appear as badges on cards and organize the whole content library. Taglines rotate through posters, social cards, and emails. CTAs are the button labels.

    The content defaults at the bottom fill the actual posters, displays, and emails in the template gallery — change a stat row and watch the “Know Your Game” poster update.

    Brand Pillars
    The promises your content makes. At least two recommended.
    ⚠ Define at least two pillars — the build validator expects them.

    Transparency-focused taglines for your “Open” pillar.
    Social Taglines

    Feature CTAs

    Jurisdiction & Support

    Configure your jurisdiction’s regulatory and support information.

    Why this matters

    Every template carries a helpline and an age line — they resolve from the values here. Picking a jurisdiction preset fills the correct helpline, self-exclusion program, support organization, minimum age, and any regulator-prescribed disclaimer wording.
