# Video Poker — Quick Reference

## How it works
You're dealt five cards from a standard deck. You choose which to keep (hold) and which to replace (draw). Your final hand determines whether you win. Unlike slots, your decisions change the math.

## The key numbers
- **Full-pay Jacks or Better (9/6):** `0.46%` house edge with optimal strategy
- **Short-pay 6/5 machine:** `5%` house edge — same game, ten times worse
- **Royal flush:** Hits roughly once every `40,000` hands

## One thing to know
Check two numbers on the paytable: the full house payout and the flush payout. On Jacks or Better, `9/6` is the best. Every step down costs you real money — and the machines look identical.

## The math in plain English
On a full-pay 9/6 machine with optimal strategy, for every `$100` you bet over time, you'd lose about `46 cents`. Drop to a 6/5 machine and that same `$100` costs you `$5`.
