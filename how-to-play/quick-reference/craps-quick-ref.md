# Craps — Quick Reference

## How it works
One player rolls two dice. The first roll (come-out) sets the game: roll 7 or 11 and Pass bets win, roll 2, 3, or 12 and they lose. Any other number becomes the "point." Keep rolling until you hit the point again (Pass wins) or roll a 7 (Pass loses).

## The key numbers
- **Pass line:** `1.41%` house edge
- **Don't Pass:** `1.36%` house edge
- **Odds bet:** `0%` — pays true mathematical odds
- **Proposition bets:** `9-17%` house edge

## One thing to know
The Odds bet is the only bet in the casino with zero house edge. After a point is set, you can place it behind your Pass or Don't Pass bet. The casino pays true odds with no margin. Most tables let you bet 3x to 5x your line bet in Odds.

## The math in plain English
Stick to Pass/Don't Pass plus Odds and you're looking at roughly `$1.40` lost per `$100` wagered over time. Start throwing chips at proposition bets in the center of the table and that jumps to `$10-$17` per `$100`. The layout is designed to make the worst bets the most visible.
