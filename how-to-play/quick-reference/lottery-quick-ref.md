# Lottery — Quick Reference

## How it works
Pick numbers, buy a ticket, wait for the draw. Every number combination has an identical chance of being selected. The draw is random — past results don't influence future ones. Most revenue goes to prizes, government programs, and operating costs.

## The key numbers
- **Major draw games (Powerball, Mega Millions):** `50-55%` RTP
- **Scratch cards:** `60-75%` RTP
- **Jackpot odds:** `1 in 100 million` to `1 in 300 million`

## One thing to know
Every number has an identical chance of being drawn. Picking "lucky" numbers, birthdays, or patterns doesn't change the odds. But picking less popular numbers means if you do win, you're less likely to split the prize with other players who chose the same combination.

## The math in plain English
Major draw games return about `50-55` cents of every dollar as prizes. For every `$100` spent on tickets over time, expect to get back `$50-$55`. Scratch cards are slightly better at `$60-$75` back per `$100`. The jackpot gets the attention, but most prize money is paid out in smaller tiers.
