# Bingo — Quick Reference

## How it works
Numbers are drawn randomly. Match them to your card. Complete the required pattern first and you win. Every card in the game has an equal chance — the draw doesn't care which card you're holding.

## The key numbers
- **House edge (online):** `5-15%`
- **House edge (in-venue):** `10-25%`
- **Your odds per card:** `1 ÷ total cards in play`

## One thing to know
Off-peak sessions have fewer players — which means better individual odds per card. If 50 people play instead of 200, your one card goes from a 1-in-200 chance to 1-in-50. The prizes are usually smaller, but the math is better.

## The math in plain English
The house edge comes from the gap between total card sales and total prizes paid out. If a session sells `$1,000` in cards and pays `$800` in prizes, the house edge is `20%`. Buying more cards in the same session improves your share of the odds but increases your total spend.
