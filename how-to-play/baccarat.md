## The 30-second version

> Baccarat is pure chance with no player decisions after the bet. You pick one of three outcomes — Banker, Player, or Tie — and the cards determine the rest. The Banker bet has a `1.06%` house edge (after commission). The Player bet has `1.24%`. Both are among the best odds in the casino. The Tie bet has a `14.4%` edge. The only decision that matters is which bet you place.

---

## How the game works

### Card values

- **2 through 9:** Face value
- **10, J, Q, K:** `0` (worth nothing)
- **Ace:** `1`

Only the last digit of the total counts. A hand of `7` + `8` = `15`, which counts as `5`. A hand of `4` + `6` = `10`, which counts as `0`. The best possible hand is `9`.

### Playing a round

1. **Place your bet.** Choose Banker, Player, or Tie. That's it — this is your only decision.
2. **Two hands are dealt.** The dealer gives two cards to the Player position and two cards to the Banker position. These are table positions, not actual people — "Player" and "Banker" are just the names of the two hands.
3. **Check for a natural.** If either hand totals `8` or `9`, it's called a natural. The round ends immediately. Naturals always win (unless both hands have naturals of equal value — that's a tie).
4. **Third card rules (automatic).** If neither hand has a natural, the dealer follows a fixed set of rules to determine whether the Player hand and then the Banker hand draw a third card. You don't make this decision. The dealer doesn't make this decision. It's automatic.
5. **Compare totals.** The hand closest to `9` wins. If they're equal, it's a tie.
6. **Payouts.** Winning Banker bets pay `1:1` minus a `5%` commission. Winning Player bets pay `1:1`. Winning Tie bets pay `8:1` (or `9:1` at some tables).

### The third card rules

You don't need to memorize these. The dealer handles them automatically. But here's the logic:

**Player hand:**
- Total `0–5`: Draw a third card
- Total `6–7`: Stand
- Total `8–9`: Natural — stand and compare

**Banker hand** (depends on the Player's third card):
- Total `0–2`: Always draw
- Total `3–6`: Draw or stand based on specific rules tied to the Player's third card
- Total `7`: Stand
- Total `8–9`: Natural — stand and compare

The Banker rules are complex precisely because they give the Banker hand a slight statistical edge. That's why winning Banker bets pay commission — the house is compensating for the Banker's better odds.

### Variants

| Variant | Key difference |
|---------|---------------|
| **Punto banco** | The standard version in most casinos worldwide. Fully automatic — no player decisions after the bet. This is the version described in this guide. |
| **Mini baccarat** | Same rules as punto banco, played at a smaller table with lower minimums and faster pace. The dealer handles all the cards. |
| **Chemin de fer** | Players take turns being the Banker and make drawing decisions. Rare outside European casinos. |
| **No-commission baccarat** | Eliminates the `5%` Banker commission but modifies the payout (e.g., Banker wins on `6` pay `0.50:1` instead of `1:1`). Check the house edge — it's usually similar or slightly worse than standard. |

## Bet types

| Bet | What it means | Payout | House edge |
|-----|--------------|--------|-----------|
| **Banker** | The Banker hand finishes closer to `9` | `1:1` minus `5%` commission | `1.06%` |
| **Player** | The Player hand finishes closer to `9` | `1:1` (no commission) | `1.24%` |
| **Tie** | Both hands finish with the same total | `8:1` (some tables pay `9:1`) | `14.4%` (at `8:1`) |

### Side bets

Many baccarat tables offer side bets — Banker Pair, Player Pair, Big, Small, and others. These have varying house edges, but they're almost always significantly higher than the main bets.

| Common side bet | Typical house edge |
|----------------|-------------------|
| **Banker Pair** | `~10.4%` |
| **Player Pair** | `~10.4%` |
| **Either Pair** | `~14.1%` |

**Key point:** The Banker and Player bets are where the math works in your favor (relatively). The Tie bet and side bets carry dramatically higher house edges. The game's reputation for good odds applies to the main bets — not to everything on the table.

## The math

### House edge by bet

| Bet | House edge | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| **Banker** | `1.06%` | Wins slightly more often (`50.68%` of decided hands) due to the third-card drawing rules. The `5%` commission on wins brings the effective edge to `1.06%`. |
| **Player** | `1.24%` | Wins `49.32%` of decided hands. No commission, but the lower win rate produces a slightly higher edge. |
| **Tie** | `14.4%` | Ties occur about `9.5%` of the time. The `8:1` payout implies `11.1%` probability. The gap between real frequency and payout odds is the house edge. |

### What this means for your wallet

<!-- ADAPT: currency -->
On Banker bets with a `1.06%` house edge: for every `$100` you bet over time, you'd lose about `$1.06` on average. On Player bets, that's `$1.24`. Both are among the best odds in any casino game.

On Tie bets at `14.4%`: that same `$100` costs you about `$14.40` over time. That's roughly `13` times worse than the Banker bet — on the same table, in the same game.
<!-- /ADAPT -->

### Ties and your main bet

When a Tie occurs and you bet Banker or Player, your main bet is returned (it's a push). This is important: the house edge figures above already account for this. You don't lose your Banker or Player bet on a tie — it's just returned.

## Key terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Natural** | A hand totaling `8` or `9` on the first two cards. The round ends immediately. The natural wins unless the other hand also has a natural of equal or higher value. |
| **Banker / Player** | The names of the two hands dealt each round. These are table positions, not real people. Betting on "Banker" doesn't mean you're betting for the house. |
| **Commission** (vig) | The `5%` fee taken from winning Banker bets. It exists because the Banker hand wins more often than the Player hand, due to the third-card rules. See [glossary](../brand-book/glossary.md#bookmaker-margin-vig--juice). |
| **Punto banco** | The standard casino version of baccarat. Fully automatic — the dealing rules are fixed. No player decisions beyond choosing your bet. |
| **Third card rule** | The fixed set of rules that determines whether the Player and Banker hands draw a third card. Handled by the dealer. |
| **Tie** (push on main bets) | When both hands have the same total. Banker and Player bets are returned. Tie bets pay `8:1` or `9:1`. |
| **Shoe** | The dealing device holding multiple decks (typically `6` or `8`). Baccarat is usually dealt from a shoe, shuffled and cut before play begins. |
| **Scorecard** | The paper card many players use to track results. Every baccarat table provides them. They record history but don't predict future hands — each hand is independent. |
| **Burn cards** | Cards discarded from the top of the shoe before dealing begins. Standard procedure, no effect on the game's odds. |
| **Mini baccarat** | A smaller, faster version of the game with the same rules and odds. The dealer handles all cards. Lower table minimums. |

## Tips for informed play

<!-- ADAPT: directness -->
1. **The Banker bet has the best odds on the table.** At `1.06%`, it's one of the lowest house edges in the casino. The `5%` commission sounds annoying, but it's already built into that number. After commission, Banker is still the best bet in baccarat.
2. **Skip the Tie bet.** The `8:1` payout looks attractive. The `14.4%` house edge isn't. You'd need the Tie to hit about `11%` of the time to break even — it hits about `9.5%`. The math doesn't work.
<!-- /ADAPT -->
3. **Scorecards are for tracking, not predicting.** Baccarat tables provide paper scorecards and electronic boards showing recent results. Many players study them for patterns. Each hand is dealt from a freshly shuffled position in the shoe — past results don't influence future outcomes. Track if you enjoy it, but know it's entertainment, not information.
4. **Side bets have dramatically higher house edges.** Pair bets, Big/Small, and other side bets run `10–15%` or more. The main game's excellent odds don't extend to the side bets. Check the house edge before placing them.
<!-- ADAPT: currency -->
5. **Set your session budget.** Baccarat moves at a moderate pace — about `40–80` hands per hour at a full table, faster at mini baccarat. At `$25` per hand, that's `$1,000–$2,000` per hour in total wagers. Decide what your session is worth before the first hand.
<!-- /ADAPT -->

## Quiz questions

### Question 1

**Stem:** Which baccarat bet has the lowest house edge?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| A | Player — because there's no commission |
| B | Banker — even after the 5% commission |
| C | Tie — because it pays 8:1 |
| D | They're all the same |

**Correct:** B

**Explanation:** The Banker hand wins slightly more often (50.68% of decided hands) because of the third-card drawing rules. Even after the casino takes a 5% commission on Banker wins, the house edge is 1.06% — lower than the Player bet's 1.24%. The commission is the price of the Banker's better odds, and it's still a good deal. The Tie bet at 14.4% isn't in the same category.

**Source:** Standard baccarat probability based on 8-deck shoe.

---

### Question 2

**Stem:** You're at a baccarat table and Banker has won 7 hands in a row. What are the odds that Banker wins the next hand?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| A | Higher than normal — Banker is on a streak |
| B | Lower than normal — Player is "due" |
| C | About the same as any other hand |
| D | 100% — seven in a row can't be a coincidence |

**Correct:** C

**Explanation:** Each baccarat hand is an independent event. The cards are dealt from a shuffled shoe, and past results don't influence future outcomes. The Banker hand will win about 50.68% of decided hands regardless of what happened in the previous 7, 70, or 700 hands. Scorecards and electronic displays show history, not predictions. Streaks feel meaningful — statistically, they're just random clustering.

**Source:** Independence of random events applied to standard baccarat dealing procedure.

---

### Question 3

**Stem:** A baccarat table offers a Tie bet paying 8:1. Ties occur about 9.5% of the time. What's the house edge on the Tie bet?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| A | About 1% — similar to Banker |
| B | About 5% — moderate |
| C | About 14.4% — more than 13 times the Banker bet's edge |
| D | 0% — the 8:1 payout perfectly matches the odds |

**Correct:** C

**Explanation:** For the Tie bet to break even, ties would need to occur about 11.1% of the time (1 in 9, matching the 8:1 payout). They actually occur about 9.5% of the time. That gap between the real probability and the implied probability is a 14.4% house edge — more than 13 times worse than the Banker bet. Same table, same game, dramatically different math depending on which bet you place.

**Source:** Tie probability from standard 8-deck baccarat simulation.
