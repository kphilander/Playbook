# Brazil (SPA/MF) -- Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is a detailed reference for gambling advertising rules in Brazil. It supplements the [Brazil compliance module](README.md). Brazil's advertising rules are prescriptive: **Lei no 14.790/2023, Art. 17** sets the statutory prohibitions, and **Portaria SPA/MF no 1.231/2024, Art. 12** enumerates them in detail (roughly nineteen distinct prohibitions). The warning-clause requirements come from **Lei 14.790/2023, Art. 16** and **Portaria 1.231/2024, Art. 13**. The legal market is online-only -- there are no land-based venues.

| Legislation | Scope |

## Required disclosures in all advertising

Every gambling advertisement (and all communication and marketing, including digital) must include:

| Disclosure | Details |
|---|---|
| **Age mark / under-18 notice** | "18+" or the notice **"proibido para menores de 18 anos"** |
| **Addiction-risk warning** | A warning about the **risks of addiction and pathological gambling** |
| **Warning size & form** | Clear and legible; **at least 10% of the ad size**; conveyed in **spoken and written** form where the medium allows |
| **Opening-page placement** | Warning clauses must appear on the **opening page** of operator sites and apps |
| **Advertising identification** | The ad must **identify as advertising** and state the **SPA authorization** |

These requirements are mandatory regardless of channel, format, or medium.

> **No fixed slogan is mandated.** There is no word-for-word required sentence (e.g. "Aposte com responsabilidade"). The binding obligation is the **set of elements** above. Confirm exact current wording and formatting with your legal team against the live Portaria text.

## The enumerated prohibitions (Art. 12 / Art. 17)

Brazilian gambling advertising must **not**:

### Claims and framing
1. Make **unfounded or misleading claims** about winning probabilities or potential gains.
2. Suggest that betting is an **easy gain** or a path to easy money.
3. Suggest that betting drives **personal, social, or financial success**.
4. Present betting as a **life priority**.
5. Present betting as an **alternative to employment**.
6. Present betting as a **source of income**.
7. Present betting as an **investment**.
8. Present betting as a **solution to financial, personal, or social problems**.

### Conduct and urgency
9. **Encourage excessive betting** or play beyond one's means.
10. Contain **calls to immediate action** / create urgency to bet now.
11. Disparage **non-betting** or portray abstaining as negative.

### Endorsements and audience
12. Use **celebrity, influencer, or authority endorsement** that suggests betting brings success or easy gain.
13. Be **directed at, target, or feature minors**.
14. **Appeal to minors** or exploit their inexperience or credulity.
15. Be directed at or exploit **vulnerable persons**.

### Content standards
16. Contain **sexual or objectifying** content.
17. Associate betting with **performance-enhancing** or otherwise irresponsible behaviour.

### Integrity and identification
18. Fail to **identify as advertising** or omit the **SPA authorization**.
19. Be directed to **self-excluded or court-excluded persons** (Art. 11).

> The exact count and grouping vary by how Art. 12's items are parsed; "roughly nineteen" is the working figure. Treat this list as the compliance baseline and verify against the live Portaria text before publishing.

## Placement and channel rules

| Channel | Key restrictions | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Broadcast (TV/radio) | Warning clauses in spoken + written form; all Art. 12 prohibitions apply | Portaria 1.231/2024, Arts. 12-13 |
| Digital / social | Warning clauses on the opening page of site/app; identify as advertising + SPA authorization; influencer rules; no targeting/featuring minors | Portaria 1.231/2024, Arts. 12-13 |
| Print / outdoor | Warning clauses >=10% of the ad; all Art. 12 prohibitions apply | Portaria 1.231/2024, Arts. 12-13 |
| Direct marketing | No advertising to self-excluded or court-excluded persons | Portaria 1.231/2024, Art. 11 |
| Sponsorship | Same Art. 12 prohibitions and warning-clause rules | Portaria 1.231/2024, Arts. 12-13 |
| In-venue | **N/A** -- no legal land-based venues in Brazil | -- |

## Prohibited content (summary)

- Unfounded/misleading claims about odds or gains
- "Easy money," success, priority, income, investment, or problem-solving framing
- Encouragement of excessive betting or urgency to act
- Endorsements suggesting betting brings success or easy gain
- Targeting, featuring, or appealing to minors; targeting vulnerable persons
- Sexual or objectifying content
- Advertising to self-excluded or court-excluded persons
- Ads that do not identify as advertising or omit the SPA authorization

---

## Enforcement

Advertising compliance sits within the SPA/MF supervisory regime for the federal market. The SPA can require corrective action and apply administrative sanctions for breaches of the Lei 14.790/2023 and Portaria 1.231/2024 advertising rules; consumer-protection and self-regulatory bodies (e.g. CONAR) also engage with gambling advertising in Brazil. Verify the current sanction framework and any self-regulatory codes with legal counsel before launch.
