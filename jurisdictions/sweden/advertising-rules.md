# Sweden -- Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is the detailed advertising reference for Sweden. It covers Sweden's prescriptive advertising framework: the Gambling Act (Spellagen 2018:1138) ch.15, the Marketing Act (Marknadsforingslagen 2008:486), Spelinspektionen's supervision, and the Consumer Agency / Consumer Ombudsman's role. Sweden's rules pair a broad statutory standard of **moderation** with specific prohibitions on bonuses beyond the first occasion of play, marketing to self-excluded players, and advertising unlicensed gambling on broadcast and video-sharing platforms. ## Sweden's advertising framework

### Sources of advertising requirements

Sweden's gambling advertising is governed by two overlapping layers:

| Layer | Source | Enforced by | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary legislation** | Gambling Act (Spellagen 2018:1138) ch.15 | Spelinspektionen | Gambling-specific advertising rules: moderation, age, support contact, self-exclusion protection, sponsorship, channel bans |
| **General consumer law** | Marketing Act (Marknadsforingslagen 2008:486) | Swedish Consumer Agency / Consumer Ombudsman (Konsumentverket / KO) | Misleading marketing, aggressive practices, the moderation assessment |
| **Implementing detail** | Gambling Ordinance (2018:1475) ch.11 | Spelinspektionen | Bonus terms (clear language, >=60 days), documentation of measures |

### Key distinction: Sweden vs. North American jurisdictions

| Feature | Sweden | Nevada | Ontario |
|---|---|---|---|
| Approach | Prescriptive + duty-of-care overlay | Principles-based | Standards-based |
| Marketing standard | Statutory "moderation" (mattfullhet) | Decency / good taste | Truthful; no targeting at-risk |
| Bonuses | Only on the first occasion of play | Clear promotion terms | Inducements prohibited in public advertising |
| Self-exclusion in ads | No marketing to self-excluded players (ch.15 §2) | Not addressed in ad rules | Removed from marketing within 24h of self-exclusion |
| Support contact in ads | Mandatory, all channels except radio | Recommended, not always mandated | ConnexOntario required on registration pages |
| Unlicensed gambling | Ads banned on TV, radio, video-sharing | Not addressed | Only registered operators may advertise |

## Gambling Act ch.15 -- core advertising rules

The Gambling Act ch.15 establishes the binding requirements for all gambling marketing in Sweden:

### What marketing must do

1. **Observe moderation** -- marketing must be carried out with moderation (ch.15 §1)
2. **Show the minimum age** -- commercial communications must include clear minimum-age (18+) information (ch.15 §3)
3. **Show a support contact** -- commercial communications, except on radio, must include contact information for an organisation that provides information about and support for problem gambling (ch.15 §3)
4. **Present winning chances accurately** -- the likelihood of winning must be correct and games must not give a false impression of a near-win (ch.14 §§4-5)

### What marketing must not do

1. **Target under-18s** -- marketing may not be directed at persons under 18 (ch.15 §1)
2. **Reach self-excluded players** -- marketing may not be directed at self-excluded players or closed accounts without opt-in (ch.15 §2)
3. **Exceed the first-occasion bonus rule** -- bonus offers may only be made on the first occasion a player participates in a game (ch.14 §9)
4. **Promote unlicensed gambling** -- advertising for gambling without a licence is banned on TV, radio, and video-sharing platforms (ch.15 §6)

### Sponsorship

Gambling sponsorship is subject to specific restrictions under ch.15 §5. Review any team, event, or kit sponsorship arrangement against these limits before committing.

*Source: [Spelinspektionen -- rules and regulation](https://www.spelinspektionen.se/en/rules-and-regulation/eng-spelansvar-omsorgsplikt/)*

## The moderation standard

The single most important advertising concept in Sweden is **moderation** (Swedish: *mattfullhet*), set out in Gambling Act ch.15 §1.

### What the statute says

Marketing of gambling to consumers must be carried out **with moderation**. In practice this means:

- No aggressive or intrusive marketing
- No exaggeration of winning chances or the benefits of gambling
- No pressure to gamble, and no presentation of gambling as a solution to problems
- No targeting of persons under 18 or of vulnerable groups

### A note on "special moderation"

Swedish courts have, in case law, interpreted the moderation requirement strictly -- a line of reasoning often summarised as **"special moderation"** (*sarskild mattfullhet*). That phrase is a **judicial interpretation**, not the literal wording of the Act. For compliance citations, refer to the statutory word **"moderation" (ch.15 §1)**; treat "special moderation" as interpretive context, not a quotable statutory standard.

### What this means for {{PROGRAM_NAME}}

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} content is informational and educational by design, which sits comfortably inside the moderation standard. The risk areas to review are tone (no hype), imagery (no glamorisation), and any odds or winnings claim (must be accurate).

## Marketing Act overlay

The Marketing Act (Marknadsforingslagen 2008:486) provides the general consumer-protection framework that overlays the gambling-specific rules. The Swedish Consumer Agency and the Consumer Ombudsman (Konsumentverket / KO) enforce it.

### Key provisions applicable to gambling

| Provision | Relevance to gambling advertising |
|---|---|
| **Misleading marketing** | Winning chances and odds must not be presented in a misleading way |
| **Aggressive marketing** | Pressure tactics and intrusive marketing are prohibited; reinforces the moderation standard |
| **The moderation assessment** | The moderation requirement is assessed against general fair-marketing principles in the Marketing Act |

### Enforcement

The Consumer Ombudsman can act against operators whose marketing breaches the Marketing Act, including by seeking injunctions and market-disruption penalties. Spelinspektionen and the Consumer Agency coordinate on gambling advertising enforcement.

## Bonus advertising rules

Sweden has a distinctive and strict rule for bonuses and promotions.

### First-occasion-only

Under Gambling Act ch.14 §9, a **bonus offer may only be made on the first occasion** a player participates in a game with the licensee. This means:

- A welcome bonus at first play is permitted
- Recurring bonuses, reload bonuses, loyalty bonuses, and re-engagement offers framed as bonuses are **not** permitted
- The rule is one of the tightest bonus restrictions in the EU and is a frequent enforcement topic

### Bonus terms

When a first-occasion bonus is offered, the Gambling Ordinance ch.11 §6 requires:

- Terms expressed in **clear and comprehensible language**
- At least **60 days** for the player to meet the bonus conditions

### {{PROGRAM_NAME}} implication

Any template that references a bonus must make clear it is a first-occasion (welcome) offer only, with clear terms and the >=60-day window. Templates that imply ongoing or repeat bonuses must be modified or not used in Sweden.

## Channel-by-channel rules

### Broadcast (TV)

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Moderation | All TV gambling marketing must observe moderation | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Disclosures | Minimum-age (18+) and problem-gambling support contact | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Unlicensed gambling | Advertising for unlicensed gambling is banned | Gambling Act ch.15 §6 |

### Radio

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Moderation | Radio gambling marketing must observe moderation | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Minimum age | Minimum-age (18+) information required | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Support contact | The support-contact obligation does **not** apply on radio (statutory exemption) | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Unlicensed gambling | Advertising for unlicensed gambling is banned | Gambling Act ch.15 §6 |

### Digital (web, social media, video-sharing)

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Moderation | All digital gambling marketing must observe moderation | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Age targeting | Must not be directed at persons under 18 | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Disclosures | Minimum-age (18+) and support contact | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Self-excluded players | Must not target self-excluded players or closed accounts without opt-in | Gambling Act ch.15 §2 |
| Unlicensed gambling | Advertising for unlicensed gambling is banned on video-sharing platforms | Gambling Act ch.15 §6 |
| Bonus ads | First-occasion only; clear terms; >=60 days | Gambling Act ch.14 §9; Ordinance ch.11 §6 |

### Direct marketing (email, SMS, push)

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Self-excluded players | Prohibited -- no gambling marketing to self-excluded players or closed accounts without opt-in | Gambling Act ch.15 §2 |
| Moderation | Same moderation standard as other channels | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Disclosures | Minimum-age and support contact | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Bonus offers | First-occasion only; clear terms | Gambling Act ch.14 §9; Ordinance ch.11 §6 |

### In-venue / point of sale (retail betting, lottery agents)

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Age notice | The age limit must be clearly stated where gambling is provided | Gambling Act ch.14 §3 |
| Support contact | Problem-gambling support contact displayed at the point of sale | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |
| Note | Land-based casinos were abolished from 1 January 2026; in-venue rules apply to remaining retail points of sale | Gambling Act |

### Sponsorship

| Rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsorship restrictions | Gambling sponsorship is subject to specific restrictions | Gambling Act ch.15 §5 |
| Moderation | Sponsorship-linked marketing must observe moderation | Gambling Act ch.15 §1 |
| Disclosures | Minimum-age and support-contact obligations apply to sponsorship communications | Gambling Act ch.15 §3 |

## Prohibited content

The following is prohibited in gambling advertising in Sweden, across channels and verticals:

1. **Marketing directed at under-18s** -- in content or media choice
2. **Marketing that fails the moderation standard** -- aggressive, intrusive, or exaggerated marketing
3. **Misleading winning chances** -- presenting odds so as to exaggerate the probability of winning; a false impression of a near-win
4. **Marketing to self-excluded players** -- any advertising directed at self-excluded players or closed accounts without opt-in
5. **Bonuses beyond the first occasion** -- any bonus offer after a player's first occasion of play
6. **Advertising unlicensed gambling** -- on TV, radio, or video-sharing platforms

### {{PROGRAM_NAME}} compliance notes

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} content is designed to be informational and educational, which reduces most prohibited-content risks. Operators should still review:

- **Tone**: Ensure entertainment framing stays within the moderation standard -- no hype, no pressure
- **Bonus references**: Any bonus mention must be first-occasion only, with clear terms and the >=60-day window
- **Odds content**: Game guides with house-edge data are compliant because they present odds accurately, not misleadingly
- **Audience targeting**: Ensure no campaign targets under-18s or self-excluded players
- **Social pillar content**: "Worth sharing" quizzes and myth-busters are compliant as educational, not promotional, material

## Required disclosures

### In every gambling commercial communication

| Disclosure | Requirement | Format guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **18+** | Minimum-age information | Clear and visible; all channels |
| **Support contact** | Problem-gambling support organisation (Stodlinjen) | All channels **except radio** |

### In bonus / promotion communications

In addition to the standard disclosures, a first-occasion bonus communication must:

| Disclosure | Requirement | Format guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **First-occasion framing** | Make clear the offer applies only at first play | In the immediate advertising context |
| **Clear terms** | Bonus conditions in clear, comprehensible language | Alongside the offer |
| **Completion window** | At least 60 days to meet the conditions | Stated with the terms |

### Disclosure placement hierarchy

1. **18+** -- always present and visible
2. **Support contact (Stodlinjen)** -- all channels except radio
3. **Bonus terms** -- if a first-occasion bonus is advertised
