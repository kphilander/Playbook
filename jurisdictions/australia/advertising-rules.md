# Australia — Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is the detailed advertising reference for Australia. It covers the federal online-wagering advertising regime — the prohibition on advertising illegal services, the live-sport timing restrictions, the Consistent Gambling Messaging mandate (seven verbatim taglines + call to action), and inducement restrictions — plus the state and broadcast layers. Use this alongside the [Australia compliance module](README.md) for complete coverage.
>
> **The vertical trap**: Online casino-style gaming and online in-play (live) betting are **illegal** in Australia and must **never** be advertised. This module is for legal online wagering (pre-event sports and race betting, lotteries, keno) and, where noted, land-based venues.

## Australia's multi-layer framework

Australian online-wagering advertising sits under several overlapping layers:

| Layer | Source | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Federal — prohibition** | Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Cth) Part 7A | Bans advertising of prohibited (illegal) interactive gambling services and unlicensed regulated services |
| **Federal — live sport** | Broadcasting Services (Online Content Service Provider Rules) 2018 (F2018L01203) Pts 3-4, s17 | Timing restrictions on gambling ads during live sport on online content services; broadcaster codes mirror this for TV/radio |
| **Federal — messaging** | NCPF National Policy Statement, Part 8 (Consistent Gambling Messaging — NCPF Measure 8) | Platform-permitted taglines (equal rotation over 12 months) + prescribed call to action |
| **Federal — inducements** | NCPF National Policy Statement (Measure 4 — restrictions on inducements) | Restriction on publicly advertised sign-up inducements |
| **State / territory — wagering ads** | NSW Betting and Racing Regulation 2022 + L&GNSW Guideline GL4015 (NSW illustrative) | Bans inducement advertising published or communicated to NSW persons; geo-disclaimers do not cure a breach; reaches influencers and social media |
| **Industry / broadcaster codes** | Free TV Commercial Television Industry Code of Practice; Commercial Radio Code of Practice 2017 (CRA); Subscription TV (ASTRA) codes; platform policies | ACMA-registered codes binding broadcast gambling ads (whistle-to-whistle, odds-promotion restriction, RG message) plus platform age-targeting |

This module focuses on the federal layers, which apply nationally and standardise online-wagering advertising conduct.

## Prohibition on advertising illegal services

Under **Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Cth) Part 7A**, it is prohibited to advertise:

- **Prohibited interactive gambling services** — including **all online casino-style gaming** (slots, table games) and **online in-play (live) betting**; and
- **Unlicensed regulated interactive gambling services** — licensed services that are not authorised under Australian law.

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| No illegal-service ads | Must not publish or broadcast advertising for a prohibited interactive gambling service | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| No unlicensed-service ads | Must not advertise an unlicensed regulated service | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| Applies across media | Covers broadcast, print, online, and other media accessible in Australia | IGA 2001 Part 7A |

> {{PROGRAM_NAME}} implication: never produce or place any creative that promotes online casino or online live-betting products for the Australian market. These products are illegal; advertising them is an offence.

## Live-sport advertising restrictions

Gambling advertising around **live sport** is tightly time-restricted ("whistle-to-whistle"). The federal **Online Content Service Provider Rules 2018 (F2018L01203)** set these for online content services; the ACMA-registered **Free TV Commercial Television Industry Code of Practice**, **Commercial Radio Code of Practice 2017 (CRA)**, and **Subscription TV (ASTRA) codes** apply equivalent rules to television and radio (see [Named broadcast and state advertising codes](#named-broadcast-and-state-advertising-codes)).

| Restriction | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Daytime ban** | No gambling advertising during live sport between **5:00 am and 8:30 pm** | F2018L01203 Pts 3-4, s17 |
| **Around-play ban** | No gambling advertising from **5 minutes before** play starts to **5 minutes after** it ends | F2018L01203 s17 |
| **Odds-promotion ban** | No commentator or representative odds promotion **30 minutes either side** of play | F2018L01203 |
| **Socially responsible** | Permitted ads must be socially responsible and carry a responsible-gambling message | F2018L01203 |

The windows are precise. Creative scheduling for live-sport contexts must account for the start/end of play and the 30-minute odds-promotion buffer, not just the broad daytime window.

## Consistent gambling messaging

Under **NCPF National Policy Statement, Part 8** (NCPF Measure 8), online wagering advertising must carry the consistent gambling messaging that replaced "Gamble Responsibly" from **30 March 2023**:

- The **mandated taglines** for the platform (drawn from the permitted subset below), displayed in **equal rotation** over a 12-month period (no single tagline favoured); and
- A **prescribed call to action** carrying the **National Gambling Helpline (1800 858 858 / gamblinghelponline.org.au)**.

### The seven taglines

| # | Tagline |
|---|---|
| 1 | Chances are you're about to lose. |
| 2 | Think. Is this a bet you really want to place? |
| 3 | What's gambling really costing you? |
| 4 | What are you prepared to lose today? Set a deposit limit. |
| 5 | Imagine what you could be buying instead. |
| 6 | You win some. You lose more. |
| 7 | What are you really gambling with? |

### Platform-specific permitted subsets

The tagline used must be permitted for the placement:

| Platform | Permitted taglines | Count |
|---|---|---|
| Television / video and radio | 1, 3, 5, 6, 7 | five |
| Digital, print, social media, website | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7 | six |
| In-app | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 | all seven |

### Verbatim call to action

> **For free and confidential support call 1800 858 858 or visit gamblinghelponline.org.au**

Radio and TV/video use approved modified forms (radio: "For free and confidential support visit gamblinghelponline.org.au"; TV/video: "For free and confidential support, call the number on screen or visit the website."). Use the platform-approved wording.

### Equal-rotation rule

Providers must take reasonable steps to ensure all **applicable** taglines (the permitted subset for the platform) are employed **equally over a 12-month period** for television, radio, in-app, digital, print, social media, and website advertising.

> **Source**: WA Consistent Gambling Messaging Notice 2025 — [cits.wa.gov.au notice](https://www.cits.wa.gov.au/department/news/news-article/2025/03/24/consistent-gambling-messaging). Taglines, platform subsets, verbatim call to action, and the 12-month equal-rotation rule were verbatim-verified against this registered state notice. The canonical Department of Social Services (DSS) consistent-gambling-messaging platform-definitions page is bot-blocked from this environment; confirm against live DSS guidance before publishing.

These are **verbatim** — they must appear word-for-word, drawn from the platform's permitted subset, and the rotation must be balanced over 12 months.

## Inducement restrictions

Under **NCPF Measure 4 (Restrictions on inducements)**, sign-up and similar inducements are restricted federally, and state wagering-ad rules layer on top:

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| No publicly advertised sign-up inducements (federal) | Online wagering providers must not publish advertising that offers an inducement to open a betting account | NCPF National Policy Statement (Measure 4) |
| NSW inducement-advertising ban (state) | It is unlawful to publish or communicate an inducement to gamble (including to open a betting account) to NSW persons. A geo-disclaimer that the inducement is "not available to NSW residents" does **not** cure the breach. The rules reach gambling influencers and social-media promotions | NSW Betting and Racing Regulation 2022; L&GNSW Guideline GL4015 |
| State overlays (other states) | Other states impose additional inducement and bonus-advertising restrictions | State gambling/advertising rules |

> {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content is informational, not promotional — it does not offer sign-up inducements, so it aligns naturally with this restriction.

> **Source**: NSW gambling-advertising rules — [nsw.gov.au gambling advertising](https://www.nsw.gov.au/business-and-economy/liquor-and-gaming/gaming/wagering-regulation/gambling-advertising) (verified). Guideline GL4015 (Gambling advertising and inducements guidelines) is issued by Liquor & Gaming NSW.

## Named broadcast and state advertising codes

The federal OCSP Rules are given practical effect — for television, radio and subscription TV — by **ACMA-registered industry codes**, and NSW adds a wagering-ad layer of its own. These are the operative standards once an ad reaches a broadcast or a NSW audience.

| Standard | Instrument | Scope | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free TV broadcast code** | Free TV Commercial Television Industry Code of Practice | Free-to-air TV gambling ads: no ads during live sport 5:00 am – 8:30 pm and 5 min before/after play; odds-promotion restriction; socially-responsible content + RG message | [freetv.com.au gambling advertising code](https://www.freetv.com.au/new-free-tv-gambling-advertising-code/) |
| **Commercial Radio code** | Commercial Radio Code of Practice 2017 (Commercial Radio Australia — CRA) | Commercial radio gambling ads: equivalent live-sport timing and RG-message obligations | [cra.au regulation and codes](https://cra.au/regulation-and-codes) |
| **Subscription TV code** | Subscription TV (ASTRA) codes of practice | Subscription/pay-TV gambling ads: equivalent live-sport timing and RG-message obligations | ASTRA codes (registered with ACMA) |
| **NSW wagering-ad layer** | NSW Betting and Racing Regulation 2022 + L&GNSW Guideline GL4015 | Bans inducement advertising published or communicated to NSW persons even behind a geo-disclaimer; reaches influencers and social media | [nsw.gov.au gambling advertising](https://www.nsw.gov.au/business-and-economy/liquor-and-gaming/gaming/wagering-regulation/gambling-advertising) |

| Category | Prohibited content | Source |
|---|---|---|

## Pending reform

> **Not law as of June 2026.** The 2023 parliamentary inquiry led by the late Peta Murphy MP ("You win some, you lose more") recommended a **phased total ban on online gambling advertising**. As of June 2026 this has **not** been legislated. Track it as a possible future reform — do **not** treat it as a current requirement, and do not cite it in compliance copy as binding. The binding rules today are the prohibition on advertising illegal services, the live-sport timing restrictions, the consistent gambling messaging mandate, and the inducement restrictions described above.
