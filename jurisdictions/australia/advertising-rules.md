# Australia — Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is the detailed advertising reference for Australia. It covers the federal online-wagering advertising regime — the prohibition on advertising illegal services, the live-sport timing restrictions, the Consistent Gambling Messaging mandate (seven verbatim taglines + call to action), and inducement restrictions — plus the state and broadcast layers. Use this alongside the [Australia compliance module](README.md) for complete coverage.
>
> **The vertical trap**: Online casino-style gaming and online in-play (live) betting are **illegal** in Australia and must **never** be advertised. This module is for legal online wagering (pre-event sports and race betting, lotteries, keno) and, where noted, land-based venues.

## Australia's multi-layer framework

Australian online-wagering advertising sits under several overlapping layers:

| Layer | Source | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Federal — prohibition** | Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Cth) Part 7A | Bans advertising of prohibited (illegal) interactive gambling services and unlicensed regulated services |
| **Federal — live sport** | Broadcasting Services (Online Content Service Provider Rules) 2018 (F2018L01203) Pts 3-4, s17 | Timing restrictions on gambling ads during live sport on online content services; broadcaster codes mirror this for TV/radio |
| **Federal — messaging** | NCPF National Policy Statement, Part 8 (Consistent Gambling Messaging) | Seven mandated taglines (equal rotation) + prescribed call to action |
| **Federal — inducements** | NCPF National Policy Statement (Measure 5) | Restriction on publicly advertised sign-up inducements |
| **State / territory** | State gambling and broadcasting/advertising rules | Additional land-based and outdoor restrictions, set per state |
| **Industry / broadcaster** | Broadcaster codes of practice; platform policies | Whistle-to-whistle commitments and platform age-targeting |

This module focuses on the federal layers, which apply nationally and standardise online-wagering advertising conduct.

## Prohibition on advertising illegal services

Under **Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Cth) Part 7A**, it is prohibited to advertise:

- **Prohibited interactive gambling services** — including **all online casino-style gaming** (slots, table games) and **online in-play (live) betting**; and
- **Unlicensed regulated interactive gambling services** — licensed services that are not authorised under Australian law.

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| No illegal-service ads | Must not publish or broadcast advertising for a prohibited interactive gambling service | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| No unlicensed-service ads | Must not advertise an unlicensed regulated service | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| Applies across media | Covers broadcast, print, online, and other media accessible in Australia | IGA 2001 Part 7A |

> {{PROGRAM_NAME}} implication: never produce or place any creative that promotes online casino or online live-betting products for the Australian market. These products are illegal; advertising them is an offence.

## Live-sport advertising restrictions

Gambling advertising around **live sport** is tightly time-restricted ("whistle-to-whistle"). The federal **Online Content Service Provider Rules 2018 (F2018L01203)** set these for online content services; broadcaster codes apply equivalent rules to television and radio.

| Restriction | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Daytime ban** | No gambling advertising during live sport between **5:00 am and 8:30 pm** | F2018L01203 Pts 3-4, s17 |
| **Around-play ban** | No gambling advertising from **5 minutes before** play starts to **5 minutes after** it ends | F2018L01203 s17 |
| **Odds-promotion ban** | No commentator or representative odds promotion **30 minutes either side** of play | F2018L01203 |
| **Socially responsible** | Permitted ads must be socially responsible and carry a responsible-gambling message | F2018L01203 |

The windows are precise. Creative scheduling for live-sport contexts must account for the start/end of play and the 30-minute odds-promotion buffer, not just the broad daytime window.

## Consistent gambling messaging

Under **NCPF National Policy Statement, Part 8**, online wagering advertising must carry the consistent gambling messaging that replaced "Gamble Responsibly" from **30 March 2023**:

- **Seven mandated taglines**, displayed in **equal rotation** (no single tagline favoured); and
- A **prescribed call to action** carrying the **National Gambling Helpline (1800 858 858 / gamblinghelponline.org.au)**.

### The seven taglines

| # | Tagline |
|---|---|
| 1 | Chances are you're about to lose. |
| 2 | Think. Is this a bet you really want to place? |
| 3 | What are you prepared to lose today? Set a deposit limit. |
| 4 | What's gambling really costing you? |
| 5 | You win some. You lose more. |
| 6 | Imagine what you could be buying instead. |
| 7 | What's gambling really costing you? Reach out. |

> **Verify before merge**: confirm the exact current list and wording of the seven taglines and the call to action against the live DSS Consistent Gambling Messaging guidance (`acma-cgm`) and the relevant state notice before publishing. The taglines here were verbatim-verified during research via a registered Western Australian Consistent Gambling Messaging Notice; the DSS explainer pages were temporarily unreachable from the research environment.

These are **verbatim** — they must appear word-for-word, and the rotation must be balanced.

## Inducement restrictions

Under **NCPF Measure 5**, sign-up and similar inducements are restricted:

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| No publicly advertised sign-up inducements | Online wagering providers must not publish advertising that offers an inducement to open a betting account | NCPF National Policy Statement (Measure 5) |
| State overlays | Some states impose additional inducement and bonus-advertising restrictions | State gambling/advertising rules |

> {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content is informational, not promotional — it does not offer sign-up inducements, so it aligns naturally with this restriction.

## Channel-by-channel rules

### Broadcast (TV and radio)

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Live-sport timing** | Whistle-to-whistle restrictions (5:00 am–8:30 pm; 5 min either side of play) | Broadcaster codes mirroring F2018L01203 |
| **Odds promotion** | No commentator/representative odds promotion 30 min either side of play | F2018L01203 |
| **Messaging** | Mandated tagline + call to action; socially responsible content | NCPF Part 8 |
| **Illegal services** | No advertising of online casino / online in-play | IGA 2001 Part 7A |

### Digital (web, app, social media)

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Online content services** | OCSP Rules apply to gambling ads on online content services, including live-sport timing | F2018L01203 |
| **Messaging** | Mandated tagline + call to action present | NCPF Part 8 |
| **Inducements** | No publicly advertised sign-up inducements | NCPF Measure 5 |
| **Illegal services** | No advertising of online casino / online in-play | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| **Age targeting** | Use platform tools to target 18+ where available | Platform policy / best practice |

### Print

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Messaging** | Mandated tagline + call to action | NCPF Part 8 |
| **Illegal services** | No advertising of online casino / online in-play | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| **Age context** | 18+ | State practice |

### Direct marketing

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **BetStop-registered persons** | Must not send electronic, telemarketing, or direct-mail marketing to registered (self-excluded) persons | IGA 2001 Part 7B |
| **Inducements** | No sign-up inducements | NCPF Measure 5 |
| **Messaging** | Include helpline call to action | NCPF Part 8 |

### In-venue (land-based — state, NSW illustrative)

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **RG signage** | Responsible-gambling and counselling information at gambling areas, cash facilities, entrances | NSW Gaming Machines Act 2001; Casino Control Act 1992 |
| **Helpline** | National Gambling Helpline displayed | NSW Gaming Machines Act 2001 |
| **State ad rules** | Outdoor and venue advertising restrictions set by the state | State gambling/advertising rules |

### Sponsorship

| Rule | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Live-sport visibility** | Gambling sponsorship visible during live sport is subject to the live-sport timing/odds restrictions | F2018L01203 / broadcaster codes |
| **Messaging** | Socially responsible; messaging where applicable | NCPF Part 8 |
| **Illegal services** | No sponsorship promoting illegal online products | IGA 2001 Part 7A |

## Prohibited content

| Category | Prohibited content | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Illegal services** | Any advertising of online casino-style gaming or online in-play betting, or of unlicensed services | IGA 2001 Part 7A |
| **Live-sport timing** | Gambling ads during live sport in restricted hours/windows; odds promotion within 30 min either side of play | F2018L01203 |
| **Inducements** | Publicly advertised sign-up inducements | NCPF Measure 5 |
| **Self-excluded persons** | Marketing directed at BetStop-registered persons | IGA 2001 Part 7B |
| **Missing messaging** | Online wagering ads without a mandated tagline and call to action | NCPF Part 8 |

## Pending reform

> **Not law as of June 2026.** The 2023 parliamentary inquiry led by the late Peta Murphy MP ("You win some, you lose more") recommended a **phased total ban on online gambling advertising**. As of June 2026 this has **not** been legislated. Track it as a possible future reform — do **not** treat it as a current requirement, and do not cite it in compliance copy as binding. The binding rules today are the prohibition on advertising illegal services, the live-sport timing restrictions, the consistent gambling messaging mandate, and the inducement restrictions described above.
