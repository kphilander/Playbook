# British Columbia — Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is the detailed advertising reference for British Columbia. As of **13 April 2026**, BC marketing is governed by binding regulation — **B.C. Reg. 215/2025 (Lottery Scheme Marketing, Advertising and Promotion Regulation)**, which **supersedes** the soft 2015 GPEB/IGCO Advertising and Marketing Standard. This reference covers that regulation, the legacy standard, the national **CGA Code for Responsible Gaming Advertising** (January 2026), channel-by-channel analysis, prohibited content, required disclosures, and a {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content compliance matrix. BC is a **Crown monopoly** — BCLC is the sole operator and advertising is centrally controlled and vetted, so there is no field of competing operators. BC's messaging is **obligation-based**: there is **no mandatory verbatim statement**, and "Know your limit, play within it." is the **BCLC/GameSense tagline**, not a regulatory requirement — though B.C. Reg. 215/2025 now mandates that ads carry a responsible-gambling message addressing prevention, mitigation, and treatment (s.6) and clearly indicate 19+ (s.7). Use this alongside the [BC compliance module](README.md) for complete coverage.

## BC's advertising framework

### Sources of advertising requirements

BC gambling advertising is governed by three overlapping layers:

| Layer | Source | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Provincial (binding)** | **B.C. Reg. 215/2025 — Lottery Scheme Marketing, Advertising and Promotion Regulation** (in force 13 April 2026) | Binding law for all provincial gaming-scheme marketing in BC; **supersedes** the 2015 standard |
| **Provincial (legacy)** | GPEB/IGCO **Advertising and Marketing Standard for Gambling in BC** (2015) | Earlier principles-based standard; superseded by B.C. Reg. 215/2025 where they overlap |
| **National** | CGA Code for Responsible Gaming Advertising (January 2026) | All gambling advertising in Canada |

Unlike Ontario, BC does **not** have an open market of private operators advertising simultaneously. **BCLC is the single operator**, so gambling advertising is centrally produced and vetted against these requirements before it runs. Service providers do not advertise gambling independently.

### Key difference from Ontario and Nevada

| Area | British Columbia | Ontario | Nevada |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Binding regulation (B.C. Reg. 215/2025) + legacy standard + national code, single operator | Standards-based (AGCO) + national code, open market | Principles-based (Reg 5.011) |
| **Mandatory statement** | None — obligation-based | None — obligation-based | None — obligation-based |
| **"Know your limit, play within it."** | BCLC/GameSense **tagline** (slogan), not a mandate | Not used as a mandate | n/a |
| **Operator model** | Crown monopoly (BCLC) — central control | Open market (70+ operators) | Competitive licensees |
| **Inducement advertising** | Centrally controlled by BCLC; no public inducement market | Sport betting inducements banned in public ads | No specific restriction |
| **National code** | CGA Code (enforceable) | CGA Code (enforceable) | AGA Code (voluntary) |

> **Correction note**: Earlier Canada modules listed BC's "Mandatory statement" as "Know your limit, play within it." That was inaccurate. BC has **no** mandatory verbatim statement; the phrase is the BCLC/GameSense **slogan**. BC's advertising regime requires a responsible-gambling **message** (no prescribed wording), like Ontario and Alberta.

## B.C. Reg. 215/2025 — binding marketing regulation

As of 13 April 2026, the **Lottery Scheme Marketing, Advertising and Promotion Regulation (B.C. Reg. 215/2025)** is the controlling authority for BC gambling marketing. It is binding law, made under the Gaming Control Act SBC 2022 c.29, and it **supersedes** the soft 2015 standard. Its requirements are stricter and more specific than the legacy standard.

| Theme | Requirement | Section |
|---|---|---|
| **Prohibited depictions** | Must not depict sexual acts, violence, trauma, problem-gambling behaviour, peer pressure to gamble, or gambling while intoxicated | s.3 |
| **No false or misleading odds** | Odds must be truthful; must not present winning as likely, or imply that skill, practice, or duration of play/spend improves the chances of winning | ss.4, 8 |
| **Mandatory RG message** | Every ad must carry a responsible-gambling message covering **prevention, mitigation, and treatment**, clearly legible or audible | s.6 |
| **19+ indication** | Marketing must clearly indicate that gambling is restricted to those **19 and older** | s.7 |
| **Athletes and celebrities banned** | Must **not** use widely-recognized athletes or celebrities in advertising on **cable, streaming, or billboards** | ss.10–11 |
| **Protecting minors** | No minors, no creative that appeals to minors, and no marketing on school grounds | ss.12–13 |
| **Self-excluded persons** | Reasonable steps to prevent marketing reaching self-excluded persons | s.14 |
| **Inducements** | Inducement terms and conditions must be disclosed; **prior written consent** required before direct inducement marketing | ss.15–16 |

*Source: [B.C. Reg. 215/2025](https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/regulationbulletin/regulationbulletin/r0215_2025).*

> The athlete/celebrity ban (ss.10–11) is the sharpest departure from the legacy standard, which only addressed endorsers whose primary appeal was to minors. Under 215/2025, a widely-recognized athlete or celebrity cannot appear in cable, streaming, or billboard ads at all — independent of whether they appeal to minors.

## Advertising & Marketing Standard (legacy)

The GPEB/IGCO **Advertising and Marketing Standard for Gambling in BC** (2015) is the **legacy** principles-based standard. It remains a useful operational reference, but where it overlaps with **B.C. Reg. 215/2025** (above), the **regulation controls**. The standard groups around three themes.

### Responsible gambling

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **RG message required** | All advertising and marketing must **contain a responsible-gambling message**. |
| **Same language** | The responsible-gambling message must be presented in the **same language** as the advertisement. |
| **No prescribed wording** | The standard requires a message — it does **not** prescribe exact words. Operators may phrase it in {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice. |

### Odds of winning

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Factual odds** | Odds of winning must be stated **factually**. |
| **No "most probable" framing** | Advertising must **not** present winning as the most probable outcome. |
| **No false expectation** | Must not imply the chances of winning increase with longer play, or that skill ensures a positive outcome. |

### Protecting minors

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **No minors / minor-appeal** | Must not use minors, or models who appear to be minors, in gambling advertising. |
| **No minor-appealing creative** | Must not use cartoon figures or content whose primary appeal is to minors. |
| **No minor-appealing endorsers** | Must not use celebrity endorsers whose primary appeal is to minors. |
| **Minor defined** | A minor is a person **under 19** in BC. |

### General content limits

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **No playing beyond means** | Must not encourage playing beyond one's means. |
| **No financial-cure framing** | Must not imply certainty of financial reward, present gambling as an alternative to employment or investment, or as a way to recover losses. |
| **No chasing losses** | Must not encourage chasing losses. |

## CGA Code for Responsible Gaming Advertising

The **CGA Code for Responsible Gaming Advertising** (January 2026), administered by Ad Standards Canada, applies nationally on top of BC's provincial standards. See [canada/README.md](../README.md#canadian-gambling-advertising-code).

| Provision | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Minors** | No advertising that targets or appeals to minors. |
| **Misleading claims** | No misleading depictions of likelihood, size, or frequency of winning. |
| **Inducements** | Bonus/inducement offers must include clear terms and conditions. |
| **Helpline** | Advertising must include a responsible-gambling message and help-line reference. |
| **Endorsers** | Endorser minimum age of 25+; influencer disclosure rules apply. |
| **Frequency** | Self-regulatory limits on ad frequency during live sports broadcasts. |

For BC, the CGA Code and the provincial standards point the same direction — a responsible-gambling message, factual odds, and strong minor protection. Where they differ in specificity (e.g., the CGA's 25+ endorser floor), apply the **stricter** requirement.

## Channel-by-channel rules

| Channel | Key restrictions | Verticals | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Broadcast (TV/cable/streaming/radio)** | RG message (prevention/mitigation/treatment) in the same language as the ad; 19+ indication; factual odds; **no widely-recognized athletes or celebrities** (cable/streaming); no minor-appealing content; CGA frequency limits during live sports | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.3–8, 10–11; CGA Code |
| **Digital** | RG message + help-line reference; 19+ indication; no minor targeting; factual odds; clear inducement T&Cs; prior written consent for direct inducement marketing | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.6–8, 15–16; CGA Code |
| **Billboards / out-of-home** | RG message; 19+; **no widely-recognized athletes or celebrities**; none on school grounds | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.10–13 |
| **Print** | RG message present; 19+ where applicable; factual odds | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.6–8 |
| **Direct marketing** | Targeted only to eligible adults; honour opt-outs; no marketing to self-excluded persons; prior written consent for direct inducements | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.14–16; RG/Internet Gambling RG Standards |

| **Sponsorship** | RG message where applicable; no minor-appealing association; **athlete/celebrity ban for cable/streaming/billboards**; CGA endorser/age rules | Both | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.10–11; CGA Code |

## Prohibited content

The following are prohibited in BC gambling advertising:

- Depicting **sexual acts, violence, trauma, problem-gambling behaviour, peer pressure to gamble, or gambling while intoxicated** (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.3).
- Using **widely-recognized athletes or celebrities** in advertising on **cable, streaming, or billboards** (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.10–11).
- Advertising that **targets or appeals to minors** (under 19) — including minor or minor-appearing models, cartoon figures, or endorsers whose primary appeal is to minors — and any **marketing on school grounds** (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.12–13).
- Presenting **winning as likely** or the most probable outcome, or stating odds non-factually (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.4, 8).
- Implying that **chances of winning increase with longer play, more practice, or greater spend**, or that **skill ensures** a positive outcome (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.8).
- Encouraging **playing beyond one's means** or **chasing losses**.
- Framing gambling as a **solution to financial problems**, an alternative to employment or investment, or a path to financial security.
- **Misleading depictions** of the likelihood, size, or frequency of winning (CGA Code).
- **Inducement offers without clear T&Cs**, or **direct inducement marketing without prior written consent** (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.15–16; CGA Code).
- Marketing directed to **self-excluded persons** (Game Break enrollees); reasonable steps must be taken to prevent it (B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.14).

## Required disclosures

| Disclosure | Format | Requirement source |
|---|---|---|
| **Responsible-gambling message** | An RG message covering **prevention, mitigation, and treatment**, clearly legible/audible, in the **same language** as the ad (no prescribed wording) | **B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.6**; legacy Advertising & Marketing Standard; CGA Code |
| **Gambling Support BC** | 1-888-795-6111 and/or gamblingsupportbc.ca | Legacy Advertising & Marketing Standard; RG Standards Pt A s.2.2 |
| **19+** | "19+" or "Must be 19+ to gamble" — marketing must clearly indicate the 19+ restriction | **B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.7**; Gaming Control Act ss.41, 43 |
| **Inducement T&Cs** | All material conditions disclosed where inducements are advertised; prior written consent for direct inducement marketing | **B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.15–16**; CGA Code |
| **Paid partnership** | Ad Standards / influencer disclosure for endorsed content (and note the athlete/celebrity ban for cable/streaming/billboards, ss.10–11) | B.C. Reg. 215/2025 ss.10–11; CGA Code |

### On-brand disclosure integration

{{PROGRAM_NAME}} treats disclosures as designed content, not fine print:

**Typical approach** (disclosures as afterthought):
> *[main ad content]*
> *^Must be 19+. Know your limit, play within it. If you have a gambling problem, call 1-888-795-6111.*

**{{PROGRAM_NAME}} approach** (disclosures as designed content):
> *[main ad content]*
>
> **19+ | Play on your terms.** Free, confidential support: **1-888-795-6111** | **gamblingsupportbc.ca**

Both satisfy BC's requirement for a responsible-gambling message and help-line reference. Under B.C. Reg. 215/2025 s.6 the message must speak to **prevention, mitigation, and treatment** and be clearly legible/audible, and s.7 requires a clear **19+** indication — so make sure the RG line points to help (treatment) and the 19+ mark is unmistakable, not buried. You **may** keep the familiar "Know your limit, play within it." GameSense line if you want to echo the program's voice — but as a stylistic choice, not because any rule compels the exact words.

### Key contrast with Ontario

BC advertising compliance differs from Ontario in two ways:
1. **Single operator** — BCLC centrally produces and vets gambling advertising; there is no open market of competing operators, so {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content flows through BCLC's review rather than many operator brands.
2. **No inducement-advertising market** — BC does not have Ontario's public sport-betting inducement ban because BC does not have a competitive inducement market to begin with; bonus prominence is centrally limited by BCLC.

What BC and Ontario share: **no mandatory verbatim statement**, a required responsible-gambling **message** (flexible wording), factual-odds rules, and strong minor protection.
