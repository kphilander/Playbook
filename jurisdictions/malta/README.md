### Upcoming changes

- The MGA's 2026 supervisory priorities emphasize enhanced regulatory oversight, evidence-driven supervision, and increased focus on thematic reviews (particularly self-exclusion practices and cryptocurrency).
- A unified self-exclusion system for remote gaming licensees has been under discussion since 2018 and may see implementation.
- The MGA continues to evolve toward an objective-based regulatory approach while maintaining its risk-based framework.

**Source**: [MGA 2026 Supervisory Priorities](https://www.mga.org.mt/mga-enhances-regulatory-oversight-and-outlines-2026-supervisory-priorities/)

### Display rules

#### Land-based display

Responsible gaming information including the RGF helpline 1777 must be accessible to patrons at all licensed gaming premises. Self-barring application forms and information must be available at the operator's reception area.

**Source**: [MGA Self-Barring FAQ](https://www.mga.org.mt/faqs/land-based-gaming-what-is-self-barring/)

#### Interactive display

Remote gaming operators must provide:
- A link to one or more gambling help organisations (within one click from anywhere on the site)
- A responsible gaming message explaining that gaming can be harmful if not controlled
- Information about all player support measures available on the website
- A link to the RGF (www.rgf.org.mt) or equivalent support organisation

**Source**: [MGA Remote Gaming RG Content FAQ](https://www.mga.org.mt/faqs/remote-gaming-what-responsible-gambling-player-protection-content-must-be-displayed-on-site/)

### On-brand helpline display

**Bare compliance** (what most operators do):
> "If you need help, call 1777."

**On-brand compliance** (the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} way):
> "Gaming should be fun -- not stressful. If it's feeling like work, the Responsible Gaming Foundation is here to help. Free. Confidential. No judgment. Call 1777 or visit rgf.org.mt."

## Messaging requirements

### Messaging regime summary

| Context | Obligation type | What's required | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Remote gaming site | Obligation-based | Responsible gaming message explaining gaming can be harmful if not controlled | PPD Art. 4(1)(b) |
| Before first deposit | Obligation-based | Information about all available RG tools and where to access them | PPD Art. 4(1)(d) |
| Underage signage | Standard-based | Sign indicating underage gaming is not permissible | PPD Art. 4(1)(a) |
| Help organisation link | Standard-based | Link to one or more gambling help organisations | PPD Art. 4(1)(e) |
| MGA seal | Standard-based | Dynamic MGA seal/kite mark displayed on site | PPD Art. 4(1)(c) |
| All advertising | Standard-based | Licensee name, licence number, minimum age, RG information | S.L. 583.09 |

**Obligation types**: No verbatim mandatory statements are prescribed by the MGA. Requirements are obligation-based (must communicate specific information) or standard-based (must display specific items).

### Verbatim required statements

None. The MGA does not prescribe word-for-word mandatory statements. Requirements are obligation-based, giving operators flexibility in wording while meeting the regulatory objective.

### Obligation-based requirements

| Obligation | What must be communicated | Where | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Responsible gaming message | Gaming can be harmful if not controlled | All remote gaming sites | PPD Art. 4(1)(b) |
| RG tools information | All available player protection tools and how to access them | Before first deposit or during registration (pop-up or attention-capturing method) | PPD Art. 4(1)(d) |
| Underage prohibition | Minors cannot gamble | All remote gaming sites; casino entrances | PPD Art. 4(1)(a) |
| Help organisation | Link to RGF or equivalent | Within one click from anywhere on the site | PPD Art. 4(1)(e) |
| No-credit rule | Operators cannot offer credit | All player-facing terms | PPD Art. 6 |

### On-brand integration

Since the MGA uses obligation-based requirements (no verbatim text), operators have full flexibility to use {{PROGRAM_NAME}} messaging.

**Generic operator approach:**
> "Gambling can be harmful. Please play responsibly. Call 1777 for help."

**{{PROGRAM_NAME}} on-brand approach:**
> "Gaming is entertainment -- set your limits, know your odds, and keep it fun. Need support? The Responsible Gaming Foundation is here: call 1777 (free, confidential) or visit rgf.org.mt."

**Rules for on-brand integration:**
1. The obligation (communicating that gaming can be harmful) must be met -- but the surrounding copy can use full {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice
2. Give all messaging visual dignity -- don't shrink to minimum size
3. Follow with a helpful action whenever possible

*Pattern from: [Messaging Framework -- Warning Statement Standards](../../brand-book/05-messaging-framework.md#warning-statement-standards)*

### Prohibited content

- Content targeting or appealing to minors or vulnerable persons
- Content suggesting gambling resolves financial, social, educational, or personal problems
- Content presenting gambling as an alternative to employment or solution to financial concerns
- Content encouraging antisocial or violent behaviour
- Content exploiting susceptibilities, aspirations, credibility, inexperience, or lack of knowledge
- Content presenting gaming as a sign of maturity or move to adulthood

### Required disclosures

All gambling advertising must include:
1. Licensee name
2. MGA licence number
3. Minimum age to participate (18+)
4. Responsible gaming information

For detailed advertising rules, see [advertising-rules.md](advertising-rules.md).

## Self-exclusion

Malta has separate self-exclusion regimes for land-based and interactive gambling.

### Land-based self-exclusion (self-barring)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Self-Barring |
| **Duration options** | 6 months, 12 months, or 12 months with auto-renewal |
| **Scope** | All land-based gambling premises in Malta |
| **Enrollment method** | In-person at operator reception, MGA offices (SmartCity, Kalkara), or Responsible Gaming Foundation offices (Fleur-de-Lys, Birkirkara). Requires completed form, proof of ID, and photograph. |
| **Reinstatement** | Cannot be removed until timeframe expires; barring is automatically removed when the period ends. Auto-renewal option can be removed by completing a Self-Barring Auto-Renew Form. |
| **Medical certificate** | Required for indefinite self-exclusion |
| **Source** | [MGA Self-Barring](https://www.mga.org.mt/player-hub/self-barring/) |

### Interactive self-exclusion

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Self-exclusion (per Player Protection Directive) |
| **Duration options** | Definite (operator-set minimum) or indefinite |
| **Scope** | Per-operator (all brands operated by the licensee if excluded for gambling problems) |
| **Account action** | Account must be closed/suspended for the exclusion period |
| **Marketing cessation** | All marketing must cease upon self-exclusion |
| **Implementation** | Requests must be implemented immediately; no attempts to induce player to continue |
| **Cross-brand rule** | If excluded due to potential gambling problems, player must be excluded from ALL brands operated by the licensee |
| **Source** | [Player Protection Directive](https://www.mga.org.mt/app/uploads/Directive-2-of-2018-Player-Protection-Directive.pdf) |

### Staff FAQ addition

Add this Q&A to the [Staff FAQ](../../collateral/customer-service/staff-faq.md):

> **Q: How does self-exclusion work in Malta?**
>
> Malta has two systems. For land-based casinos, it is called "self-barring" and covers all gambling premises in Malta. Players can self-bar for 6 months, 12 months, or 12 months with auto-renewal by visiting the casino reception, MGA offices, or the Responsible Gaming Foundation. For online gaming, players can self-exclude directly with the operator for a definite or indefinite period. If excluded for gambling problems, the exclusion covers all brands operated by that licensee. If a player asks about it, explain the options and offer to help them get started. Use "take a break" in casual conversation, "self-barring" or "self-exclusion" when referring to the specific programs.
