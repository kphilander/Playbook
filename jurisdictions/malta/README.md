## Regulatory authority

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Primary regulator** | Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) |
| **Website** | [mga.org.mt](https://www.mga.org.mt/) |
| **Governing legislation** | Gaming Act (Cap. 583, Laws of Malta, 2018) |
| **Key directives** | Player Protection Directive (Directive 2 of 2018, V3 January 2023); Gaming Authorisations and Compliance Directive (Directive 3 of 2018); Gaming Commercial Communications Regulations (S.L. 583.09) |
| **License types** | B2C (land-based casino, remote gaming); B2B (critical gaming supply) |
| **Regulatory approach** | Prescriptive / risk-based with outcome-focused supervision |

**Source**: [MGA Regulatory Framework](https://www.mga.org.mt/our-work/regulatory-framework/)

### Upcoming changes

- The MGA's 2026 supervisory priorities emphasize enhanced regulatory oversight, evidence-driven supervision, and increased focus on thematic reviews (particularly self-exclusion practices and cryptocurrency). Cross-brand self-exclusion enforcement is an explicit 2026 supervisory priority.
- In 2025 the MGA published its **Thematic Review on Self-Exclusion Practices in the Online Gaming Sector** (reviewing 20 licensees across 58 URLs, including mystery-shopping). This is now the operative supervisory expectation for how remote licensees design and enforce self-exclusion -- see the [Self-exclusion](#self-exclusion) section. A unified self-exclusion system spanning remote licensees has been discussed since 2018; until it is in force, the cross-brand exclusion duty under the Player Protection Directive (Art. 11) governs.
- The MGA continues to evolve toward an objective-based regulatory approach while maintaining its risk-based framework.

**Sources**: [MGA 2026 Supervisory Priorities](https://www.mga.org.mt/mga-enhances-regulatory-oversight-and-outlines-2026-supervisory-priorities/) | [MGA Thematic Review on Self-Exclusion Practices](https://www.mga.org.mt/the-mga-publishes-findings-from-thematic-review-on-self-exclusion-practices-in-the-online-gaming-sector/)

### Display rules

#### Land-based display

Responsible gaming information including the RGF helpline 1777 must be accessible to patrons at all licensed gaming premises. Self-barring application forms and information must be available at the operator's reception area.

**Source**: [MGA Self-Barring FAQ](https://www.mga.org.mt/faqs/land-based-gaming-what-is-self-barring/)

#### Interactive display

Remote gaming operators must provide:
- A link to one or more gambling help organisations (within one click from anywhere on the site)
- A responsible gaming message explaining that gaming can be harmful if not controlled
- Information about all player support measures available on the website
- A link to the RGF (www.rgf.org.mt) or equivalent support organisation

**Source**: [MGA Remote Gaming RG Content FAQ](https://www.mga.org.mt/faqs/remote-gaming-what-responsible-gambling-player-protection-content-must-be-displayed-on-site/)

### Messaging regime summary

| Context | Obligation type | What's required | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Remote gaming site | Obligation-based | Responsible gaming message explaining gaming can be harmful if not controlled | PPD Art. 4 |
| Before first deposit | Obligation-based | Information about all available RG tools and where to access them | PPD Art. 5(1)(a) |
| Underage signage | Standard-based | Sign indicating underage gaming is not permissible | PPD Art. 4 |
| Help organisation link | Standard-based | Link to one or more gambling help organisations | PPD Art. 4 |
| MGA seal | Standard-based | Dynamic MGA seal/kite mark displayed on site | PPD Art. 5(1)(a) |
| All advertising | Standard-based | Licensee name, licence number, minimum age, RG information | S.L. 583.09 |

**Obligation types**: No verbatim mandatory statements are prescribed by the MGA. Requirements are obligation-based (must communicate specific information) or standard-based (must display specific items).

### Verbatim required statements

None. The MGA does not prescribe word-for-word mandatory statements. Requirements are obligation-based, giving operators flexibility in wording while meeting the regulatory objective.

### Obligation-based requirements

| Obligation | What must be communicated | Where | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Responsible gaming message | Gaming can be harmful if not controlled | All remote gaming sites | PPD Art. 4 |
| RG tools information | All available player protection tools and how to access them | Before first deposit or during registration (pop-up or attention-capturing method) | PPD Art. 5(1)(a) |
| Underage prohibition | Minors cannot gamble | All remote gaming sites; casino entrances | PPD Art. 4 |
| Help organisation | Link to RGF or equivalent | Within one click from anywhere on the site | PPD Art. 4 |
| Player support channels | Player-support function reachable via online form or personal contact, including live chat or telephone | All remote gaming sites | PPD Art. 19 |
| No-credit rule | Operators cannot offer credit | All player-facing terms | PPD Art. 26 |

### On-brand integration

Since the MGA uses obligation-based requirements (no verbatim text), operators have full flexibility to use {{PROGRAM_NAME}} messaging.

**Generic operator approach:**
> "Gambling can be harmful. Please play responsibly. Call 1777 for help."

**{{PROGRAM_NAME}} on-brand approach:**
> "Gaming is entertainment -- set your limits, know your odds, and keep it fun. Need support? The Responsible Gaming Foundation is here: call 1777 (free, confidential) or visit rgf.org.mt."

**Rules for on-brand integration:**
1. The obligation (communicating that gaming can be harmful) must be met -- but the surrounding copy can use full {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice
2. Give all messaging visual dignity -- don't shrink to minimum size
3. Follow with a helpful action whenever possible

### Prohibited content

- Content targeting or appealing to minors or vulnerable persons
- Content suggesting gambling resolves financial, social, educational, or personal problems
- Content presenting gambling as an alternative to employment or solution to financial concerns
- Content encouraging antisocial or violent behaviour
- Content exploiting susceptibilities, aspirations, credibility, inexperience, or lack of knowledge
- Content presenting gaming as a sign of maturity or move to adulthood

All gambling advertising must include:
1. Licensee name
2. MGA licence number
3. Minimum age to participate (18+)
4. Responsible gaming information

## Self-exclusion

Malta has separate self-exclusion regimes for land-based and interactive gambling.

### Land-based self-exclusion (self-barring)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Self-Barring |
| **Duration options** | 6 months, 12 months, or 12 months with auto-renewal |
| **Scope** | All land-based gambling premises in Malta |
| **Enrollment method** | In-person at operator reception, MGA offices (SmartCity, Kalkara), or Responsible Gaming Foundation offices (Fleur-de-Lys, Birkirkara). Requires completed form, proof of ID, and photograph. |
| **Reinstatement** | Cannot be removed until timeframe expires; barring is automatically removed when the period ends. Auto-renewal option can be removed by completing a Self-Barring Auto-Renew Form. |
| **Medical certificate** | Required for indefinite self-exclusion |
| **Source** | [MGA Self-Barring](https://www.mga.org.mt/player-hub/self-barring/) |

### Interactive self-exclusion

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Self-exclusion (per Player Protection Directive, Art. 11) |
| **Duration options** | Definite (operator-set minimum) or indefinite; players may also self-exclude from specific products |
| **Scope** | Per-operator (all brands operated by the licensee where there are indications of disordered or problem gambling) |
| **Account action** | Account must be closed/suspended for the exclusion period |
| **Marketing cessation** | All marketing must cease upon self-exclusion; no commercial communications to self-excluded persons (extends to affiliates -- see [advertising-rules.md](advertising-rules.md)) |
| **Implementation** | Requests must be implemented immediately via a simple procedure; no attempts to induce a self-excluding player to continue (no inducements, free spins, or bonuses to a player who has signalled intent to stop) |
| **Cross-brand rule** | Where there are indications of disordered or problem gambling, the operator must ask whether the exclusion is to cover all of the licensee's brands and must apply it across all brands accordingly |
| **Current supervisory expectation** | The MGA's 2025 Thematic Review on Self-Exclusion Practices is the operative benchmark for how remote licensees present and enforce self-exclusion; cross-brand enforcement is a 2026 supervisory priority |
| **Source** | [Player Protection Directive (Art. 11)](https://www.mga.org.mt/app/uploads/Directive-2-of-2018-Player-Protection-Directive.pdf) | [MGA Thematic Review on Self-Exclusion Practices](https://www.mga.org.mt/the-mga-publishes-findings-from-thematic-review-on-self-exclusion-practices-in-the-online-gaming-sector/) |

### Staff FAQ addition

Add this Q&A to the [Staff FAQ](../../collateral/customer-service/staff-faq.md):

> **Q: How does self-exclusion work in Malta?**
>
> Malta has two systems. For land-based casinos, it is called "self-barring" and covers all gambling premises in Malta. Players can self-bar for 6 months, 12 months, or 12 months with auto-renewal by visiting the casino reception, MGA offices, or the Responsible Gaming Foundation. For online gaming, players can self-exclude directly with the operator for a definite or indefinite period, and can choose specific products. Where there are indications of gambling harm, the operator asks whether the exclusion should cover all of its brands and applies it across all of them. If a player asks about it, explain the options and offer to help them get started. Use "take a break" in casual conversation, "self-barring" or "self-exclusion" when referring to the specific programs.

| Deposit limits | Yes (one of deposit or wagering) | Daily, weekly, or monthly deposit limits. Must be offered before first deposit or during registration. ### Staff and key-function obligations (integrity-adjacent)

Beyond player-facing RG tools, the Gaming Authorisations and Compliance Directive (Directive 3 of 2018, reg. 43) requires licensees offering betting to report suspicious betting through the MGA's **Suspicious Betting Reporting Mechanism** and to appoint a **Sports Integrity Point of Contact**. This is primarily a sports-integrity obligation rather than player-facing RG communication, but it shares the staff and key-function training surface and is worth noting when scoping staff procedures.

**Source**: [MGA Updated Measures on Sports Betting Reporting and Integrity](https://www.mga.org.mt/updated-measures-sports-betting-reporting-integrity/)

## Compliance checklist

Complete before launching {{PROGRAM_NAME}} in Malta.

### Regulatory
- [ ] Identified primary regulator: Malta Gaming Authority (MGA)
- [ ] Confirmed licence requirements and status (B2C or B2B)
- [ ] Verified legal gambling age: 18+ (online), 25+ (casino for Maltese citizens)
- [ ] Reviewed permitted products table
- [ ] Checked for pending regulatory changes (2026 supervisory priorities)
- [ ] Identified deployment verticals: [ ] Land-based [ ] Interactive [ ] Both

### Messaging
- [ ] Obligation-based RG message displayed (gaming can be harmful if not controlled)
- [ ] Pre-deposit RG tools information provided (pop-up or attention-capturing method)
- [ ] Helpline 1777 displayed with link to RGF
- [ ] MGA dynamic seal/kite mark displayed
- [ ] Underage gaming sign displayed (18+)
- [ ] Age notice on all player-facing content
- [ ] All `{{PLACEHOLDER}}` tokens resolve to Malta values

### Advertising
- [ ] All ads include licensee name, licence number, 18+, RG information
- [ ] RG message is prominent; where an RG-microsite reference substitutes, it occupies at least 10% of the ad and the RG portal is no more than two clicks away (CCC Regs. 17-18)
- [ ] Minimum-age sign displayed for the entire duration of the ad (CCC Reg. 19)
- [ ] No marketing to self-excluded persons, including via affiliates
- [ ] Social-media placements use age-gating tools (CCC Reg. 13)
- [ ] Sponsorship rules observed (no product placement; none where the activity mainly involves minors; no merchandising aimed at minors) (CCC Regs. 23-28)
- [ ] No ads in public places (unless tourist areas/airports/hotels exemption applies)
- [ ] No targeting minors or vulnerable persons
- [ ] No content suggesting gambling solves financial/social problems
- [ ] Broadcast ads not placed during/adjacent to children's programmes
- [ ] Promotional schemes clearly outline wagering conditions

### Self-exclusion
- [ ] Land-based: self-barring program integrated (6mo/12mo/12mo auto-renew)
- [ ] Interactive: self-exclusion with account closure implemented (definite/indefinite/product-specific)
- [ ] Cross-brand exclusion applied where there are indications of gambling harm; player asked whether exclusion covers all brands (PPD Art. 11)
- [ ] Self-excluded players removed from all marketing immediately (including via affiliates)
- [ ] Self-exclusion presentation reviewed against the MGA 2025 Thematic Review on Self-Exclusion Practices
- [ ] Staff trained on self-barring/self-exclusion enrollment and enforcement

### Player protection -- land-based
- [ ] RG information posted at all gaming premises
- [ ] RGF helpline 1777 displayed
- [ ] Employee training program implemented
- [ ] Age verification at casino entry (25+ Maltese, 18+ non-Maltese)
- [ ] No credit offered to players

### Player protection -- interactive
- [ ] Deposit limits OR wagering limits implemented (both encouraged); limits considered realistic to income/wealth; 24-hour delay on loosening (PPD Art. 16)
- [ ] Reality checks offered (alert, suspend play, confirm-read) (PPD Art. 18A)
- [ ] RG tools information displayed before first deposit (PPD Art. 5(1)(a))

- [ ] Player-support function reachable via live chat or telephone (PPD Art. 19)
- [ ] Self-exclusion available (definite, indefinite, and product-specific); no inducement to continue (PPD Art. 11)
- [ ] Single account enforcement
- [ ] Behavioural monitoring using markers of harm; intervention triggers documented (PPD Art. 17, 2023 amendments)
- [ ] No credit offered to players (PPD Art. 26)
