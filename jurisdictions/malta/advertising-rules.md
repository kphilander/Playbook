# Malta (MGA) -- Advertising Rules

> **Operator note**: This is a detailed reference for gambling advertising rules in Malta. It supplements the [Malta compliance module](README.md). Malta's advertising rules are governed primarily by the Gaming Commercial Communications Regulations (S.L. 583.09) and enforced by the MGA's Commercial Communications Committee (CCC).

> **Last verified**: 2026-06-08

---

## Governing legislation

| Legislation | Scope |
|---|---|
| Gaming Commercial Communications Regulations (S.L. 583.09) | Primary advertising rules for all gambling operators |
| Commercial Communications Committee Guidelines (March 2019 v1, published August 2019) | Standard of practice -- 31 numbered regulations operationalising S.L. 583.09 |
| Requirements as to Advertisements, Methods of Advertising and Directions Applicable to Gambling Advertisements (S.L. 350.25) | Broadcast media timing restrictions |
| Gaming Act (Cap. 583, 2018) | Overarching legal authority |

**Sources**: [MGA Advertising FAQ](https://www.mga.org.mt/faqs/what-is-required-when-advertising/) | [CCC Guidelines PDF](https://www.mga.org.mt/app/uploads/Commercial-Communications-Committee-Guidelines.pdf) | [MGA CCC announcement and guidelines](https://www.mga.org.mt/mga-sets-up-a-commercial-communications-committee-and-publishes-guidelines/)

> **Verify before legal sign-off**: the CCC Guidelines PDF is the binding standard-of-practice source. The regulation numbers cited below were verified against the canonical MGA CCC PDF (it does not render in web-fetch tools, but it reads cleanly with a PDF text extractor); re-confirm them against that PDF before legal sign-off.

## Required disclosures in all advertising

Every gambling advertisement must include:

| Disclosure | Details |
|---|---|
| **Licensee name** | Full legal name of the licensed operator |
| **MGA licence number** | The operator's current MGA licence number |
| **Minimum age** | "18+" (or "25+" for land-based casino ads targeting Maltese citizens) |
| **Responsible gaming information** | RG messaging and/or helpline reference |

These disclosures are mandatory regardless of channel, format, or medium.

### Responsible-gaming message prominence (the "10% rule")

The CCC Guidelines require more than mere presence of an RG message -- they set its prominence:

| Standard | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **RG message prominence** | An educational responsible-gaming message must be prominently included in all commercial communications | CCC Regs. 17-18 |
| **10% substitution rule** | Where a reference to an RG microsite/portal substitutes for the full message, that reference must amount to a minimum of **10%** of the advertising space or duration | CCC Regs. 17-18 |
| **Two-click rule** | The RG web portal must be no more than **two clicks** away from the commercial communication | CCC Regs. 17-18 |
| **Age-sign duration** | The minimum-age sign must be displayed for the **entire duration** of the advertisement and be easily seen | CCC Reg. 19 |

## Placement restrictions

### Public places ban

Malta has a broad prohibition on gambling advertising in public places:

| Location | Permitted? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Public streets / squares | No | General public places prohibition |
| Public transport | No | Buses, ferries, etc. |
| Billboards | No | Unless in exempt locations |
| Airports | Yes | Tourist-frequented location exemption |
| Hotels | Yes | Tourist-frequented location exemption |
| Holiday complexes | Yes | Tourist-frequented location exemption |
| Newspapers / magazines | Yes | Exempt from public places ban |
| Authorised gaming premises | Yes | Within the premises |
| Digital (online) | Yes | Subject to content rules |

### Broadcast media restrictions

Under S.L. 350.25:

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| Children's programmes | No gambling ads during, immediately before, or immediately after children's programmes |
| Time restrictions | Specific timing rules apply to local broadcast media |

## Prohibited content

The following content is prohibited in all gambling advertising:

### Targeting and audience

- Must not be directed towards minors or vulnerable persons
- Must not encourage minors or vulnerable persons to play
- Must not feature minors in any way
- Must not appeal to minors or vulnerable persons
- Must not exploit the susceptibilities, aspirations, credibility, inexperience, or lack of knowledge of minors or vulnerable persons
- Must not present gaming as a sign of maturity or a move to adulthood

### Behaviour and claims

- Must not portray, condone, or encourage behaviour that is criminal or socially irresponsible
- Must not portray behaviour that could lead to financial, social, or emotional harm
- Must not directly or indirectly encourage antisocial or violent behaviour
- Must not suggest that gaming can be a resolution to social, educational, professional, or personal problems
- Must not present gambling as an alternative to employment
- Must not present gambling as a solution to financial concerns
- Must not suggest that solitary gaming is preferable to social gaming (CCC Reg. 6(g))
- Must not exploit cultural beliefs or traditions about gaming or luck -- for example lucky charms or icons (CCC Reg. 6(k))

### Promotional integrity

- Promotional schemes must be transparent
- Wagering conditions and obligations must be clearly outlined
- Bonus terms must be prominently displayed and not misleading

## CCC Guidelines -- additional standards of practice

The Commercial Communications Committee Guidelines (March 2019 v1, published August 2019) contain concrete, testable standards beyond the high-level prohibitions in S.L. 583.09. The most operationally significant for {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content:

### Self-excluded persons and affiliates

| Standard | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **No marketing to self-excluded persons** | Commercial communications must not be sent to persons who have self-excluded; this is an absolute prohibition | CCC Guidelines (unsolicited communications and self-excluded players) |
| **Affiliates in scope** | Affiliates acting for the licensee are expressly within scope and are jointly liable; the licensee remains responsible for communications issued on its behalf | CCC Guidelines |

### No inducement to continue

| Standard | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **No inducement** | No free spins, bonuses, or freebies (tangible or intangible) may be used to induce a player who has signalled an intention to stop gaming | CCC Reg. 10 |

This mirrors the Player Protection Directive's no-inducement rule on self-exclusion (PPD Art. 11); see the [Malta compliance module -- Self-exclusion](README.md#self-exclusion).

### Social media

| Standard | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Age-gating** | Authorised persons advertising on social platforms must use the age-gating tools those platforms provide | CCC Reg. 13 |

### Sponsorship

| Standard | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **No product placement** | Sponsorship is limited to text, logo, or imagery identifying the operator and must exclude product placement | CCC Reg. 21 |
| **Activities involving minors** | Sponsorship is prohibited where the sponsored activity mainly involves minors or vulnerable persons | CCC Reg. 24 |
| **No sponsorship of minors** | Operators must not sponsor minors (or persons of legal gaming age portrayed as minors) | CCC Reg. 25 |
| **No merchandising aimed at minors** | Merchandising connected to sponsorship must not target minors | CCC Reg. 26 |
| **No advertising in programmes appealing to minors** | Sponsorship of, or advertising in, televised programmes with particular appeal to minors or vulnerable persons is prohibited | CCC Reg. 27 |

## Enforcement

### Commercial Communications Committee (CCC)

The MGA established the CCC to review commercial communications issued by gaming operators and assess potential breaches.

| Enforcement action | Details |
|---|---|
| **Modification order** | MGA may order modification of non-compliant advertising |
| **Retraction order** | MGA may order retraction of advertising |
| **Termination order** | MGA may order termination of advertising campaign |
| **Suspension** | MGA may instruct the licensee to suspend an advertisement pending final decision |
| **Administrative penalty** | Up to EUR 25,000 per breach |
| **Daily penalty** | Up to EUR 500/day for continuing breaches |
| **Additional sanctions** | Any other administrative action deemed necessary |
