# Pennsylvania -- Compliance Module

> **Operator note**: This module covers every compliance requirement for deploying {{PROGRAM_NAME}} in Pennsylvania. Work through each section, complete the compliance checklist at the bottom, and get legal/compliance sign-off before launch. Pennsylvania uses a prescriptive model with specific advertising font size requirements, a verbatim helpline message for interactive gaming, and detailed player protection tool mandates. Pennsylvania has separate self-exclusion programs for casino and interactive gaming. Pennsylvania is the largest US online gambling revenue state, with all verticals legal: land-based casino, online casino (iGaming), online poker, and sports betting.

### Structure

The PGCB is an independent state agency with seven commissioners. Unlike Nevada's two-tier model, the PGCB combines investigative, decision-making, and enforcement functions in a single body. Key divisions relevant to {{PROGRAM_NAME}}:

| Division | Relevance to {{PROGRAM_NAME}} |
|---|---|
| **OCPG** (Office of Compulsive and Problem Gambling) | Approves signage messages, oversees self-exclusion programs, can order ad discontinuation |
| **Bureau of Gaming Operations** | Reviews promotions and tournament filings |
| **Office of Sports Wagering** | Reviews sports wagering contest and tournament filings |

## Legal requirements

| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| **Minimum gambling age** | 21+ (all gambling products) |
| **Legal framework** | State-licensed operators under 4 Pa.C.S. |
| **Online gambling** | Online casino (iGaming) legal since 2019; online poker legal; sports betting legal since 2018 |
| **Lottery** | Yes (Pennsylvania Lottery, separate from PGCB jurisdiction) |

### Permitted products

| Product | Vertical | Legal status | Regulator | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Casino (slots, table games) | Land-based | Legal | PGCB | 16+ casinos: Category 1 (racetrack casinos), Category 2 (stand-alone), Category 3 (resort), Category 4 (mini-casinos) |
| Sports betting | Land-based + Interactive | Legal | PGCB | Retail and mobile; authorized by Act 42 of 2017 |
| Online casino (iGaming) | Interactive | Legal | PGCB | Slots + table games; authorized by Act 42 of 2017; live since July 2019 |
| Online poker | Interactive | Legal | PGCB | Part of iGaming authorization |
| Video Gaming Terminals (VGTs) | Land-based | Legal | PGCB | Truck stops and qualified establishments |
| Lottery | Both | Legal | PA Lottery (separate) | Not under PGCB jurisdiction |
| Horse racing (pari-mutuel) | Land-based | Legal | PA Horse Racing Commission | Separate regulatory body |
| Fantasy sports contests | Interactive | Legal | PGCB | Regulated under Chapter 1208a |

### Licensing model

Pennsylvania uses a **category-based licensing system** for casinos:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Category 1** | Racetrack casinos (slots + table games at horse racing tracks) |
| **Category 2** | Stand-alone casinos |
| **Category 3** | Resort casinos (at qualified resorts) |
| **Category 4** | Mini-casinos (satellite facilities, max 750 slots + 30 table games) |

Interactive gaming and sports wagering certificates are issued to holders of Category 1-3 licenses, who may partner with operators.

### Display rules

#### Land-based display (501a.5; 501a.7)

- **Minimum 20 signs** with OCPG-approved gambling assistance message
- Signs required **within 50 feet** of each entrance and exit
- Signs required **above or below cash dispensing openings** on ATMs, voucher/coupon redemption machines, and other cash-dispensing equipment
- Message text must be approved by the OCPG Director

#### Interactive display (814a.1)

Verbatim message required on login/logout screens:

> **"IF YOU OR SOMEONE YOU KNOW HAS A GAMBLING PROBLEM, HELP IS AVAILABLE, CALL 1-800-GAMBLER"**

*(or Board-approved equivalent)*

Player protection pages must also include direct links to:
- Council on Compulsive Gambling of Pennsylvania website
- DDAP gambling addiction treatment provider list
- PGCB Office of Compulsive and Problem Gaming webpage

### On-brand helpline display

**Bare compliance** (what most operators do):

**On-brand compliance** (the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} way):

## Self-exclusion

Pennsylvania operates **separate self-exclusion programs** for casino and interactive gaming:

### Casino self-exclusion (58 Pa. Code Chapter 503a)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Casino Self-Exclusion |
| **Duration options** | 1 year, 5 years, or lifetime |
| **Scope** | Statewide -- all licensed facilities and Board-approved gaming locations |
| **Enrollment method** | Electronic (PGCB website), or in-person at PGCB offices (Harrisburg or regional) or licensed facilities |
| **Contact** | OCPG at (717) 346-8300 or problemgambling@pa.gov |
| **Marketing cessation** | Casinos must ensure excluded persons do not receive junket solicitations, targeted mailings, telemarketing promotions, player club materials, or other promotional materials |
| **Winnings forfeiture** | Winnings during exclusion remitted to the Board for compulsive and problem gambling programs |
| **Reinstatement (1/5 year)** | Request removal after period expires; Board removes within 5 business days; 7-business-day cooling-off before gaming floor access |
| **Reinstatement (lifetime)** | Petition after 10 years with problem gambling assessment completion and treatment provider verification |
| **Source** | [58 Pa. Code 503a](https://www.pacodeandbulletin.gov/Display/pacode?file=/secure/pacode/data/058/chapter503a/chap503atoc.html) |

### Interactive gaming self-exclusion (58 Pa. Code Chapter 815a)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Interactive Gaming Self-Exclusion |
| **Duration options** | 1 year, 5 years, or lifetime |
| **Scope** | All interactive gaming and sports wagering in Pennsylvania |
| **Account action** | Operators must refuse wagers, deny gaming privileges, and stop all marketing |
| **Marketing cessation** | No targeted mailings, telemarketing promotions, player club materials, or promotional materials relating to interactive gaming |
| **Winnings forfeiture** | Winnings remitted to the Board for compulsive and problem gambling programs |
| **Reinstatement (1/5 year)** | Automatic removal upon period completion |
| **Reinstatement (lifetime)** | Petition after 10 years with problem gambling assessment and treatment documentation |
| **Source** | [58 Pa. Code 815a](https://www.pacodeandbulletin.gov/Display/pacode?file=/secure/pacode/data/058/chapter815a/chap815atoc.html) |

### Sports wagering self-exclusion (58 Pa. Code 1410a.1)

Sports wagering is covered through the **cross-reference provisions** in 1410a.1, which requires sports wagering operators to comply with the compulsive and problem gambling provisions of Subparts I (land-based, including 503a) and L (interactive, including 815a). Sports bettors enroll through either the casino or interactive gaming self-exclusion program depending on whether they use retail or mobile.

### Staff FAQ addition

Add this Q&A to the [Staff FAQ](../../collateral/customer-service/staff-faq.md):

> **Q: How does self-exclusion work in Pennsylvania?**
>
> Pennsylvania has two separate self-exclusion programs: one for casinos (all licensed facilities statewide) and one for interactive gaming (online casino, online poker, and sports betting). Players choose 1-year, 5-year, or lifetime exclusion. They can enroll online through the PGCB website, in person at Board offices, or at a licensed facility. During exclusion, they cannot enter gaming floors or access online accounts. Any winnings during exclusion are forfeited to the Board to fund compulsive gambling programs. If a player asks about it, explain the options and offer to help them get started. Use "take a break" in casual conversation, "self-exclusion" when referring to the specific program.
