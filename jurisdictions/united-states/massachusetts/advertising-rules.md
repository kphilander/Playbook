## Massachusetts's advertising framework

Massachusetts splits advertising regulation along its dual-statute lines:

| Layer | Source | Nature | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sports wagering advertising** | 205 CMR 256 (under M.G.L. c. 23N) | Prescriptive — detailed content, targeting, font, endorsement, and record rules | Retail + mobile sports betting |
| **Casino advertising** | 205 CMR 138 internal controls (under M.G.L. c. 23K) | Obligation-based — post RG information per Commission-approved plan; no separate prescriptive ad code | Land-based casino |

205 CMR 256 is among the **most prescriptive advertising codes in the US**. The remainder of this reference focuses on it; casino advertising is covered briefly under [Casino advertising](#casino-advertising).

## Scope & operator responsibility

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Operator responsible for all advertising** | The operator is responsible for ALL advertising on its behalf, including by agents and third parties | 256.01(1) |
| **Financial relationships disclosed** | Material financial relationships must be disclosed | 256.01(4) |
| **No revenue-sharing affiliates** | Revenue-sharing affiliate arrangements are prohibited | 256.01(3) |
| **Record retention** | All advertising records retained for 6 years | 256.10 |
| **Enforcement** | After violations, the Commission may require pre-approval of advertising | 256.11(3) |

## Prohibited content

Under **205 CMR 256.04**, sports wagering advertising must not:

| Prohibition | Source |
|---|---|
| Be unfair or deceptive | 256.04(1) |
| Fail to conspicuously specify material conditions | 256.04(3) |
| Advise or encourage specific wagers | 256.04(4) |
| Describe as "free" any promotion that requires the patron to risk money | 256.04(5), 256.04(6)(d) |
| Promote irresponsible or excessive participation | 256.04(6)(a) |
| Suggest gambling guarantees success | 256.04(6)(b) |
| Imply gambling is risk-free | 256.04(6)(c) |
| Encourage chasing losses | 256.04(6)(e) |
| Suggest gambling solves financial or personal problems | 256.04(6)(f) |
| Portray gambling as a rite of passage | 256.04(6)(g) |
| Advertise on responsible gambling websites | 256.04(6)(j) |

These map cleanly onto the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice — the brand never promises wins, never frames gambling as an escape from problems, and never hides the terms. Compliance here is the floor; on-brand content clears it comfortably.

## Minor protection

Under **205 CMR 256.05**, sports wagering advertising must:

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **State 21+** | Include the 21+ age statement | 256.05(1) |
| **Not target under-21** | Must not target persons under 21 | 256.05(2) |
| **Avoid youth-appealing content** | No imagery, symbols, or language designed to appeal primarily to under-21 | 256.05(3) |
| **Respect the audience threshold** | 25% audience threshold for media outlets; must use age-exclusion controls | 256.05(4)(a) |
| **Avoid campuses** | No advertising on school or college campuses | 256.05(4)(d-e) |
| **Limit branded merchandise** | No products, clothing, or toys with betting logos designed for under-21 | 256.05(5) |

## Responsible gaming in advertising

**205 CMR 256.06** ("Advertising to Other Vulnerable Persons") is the responsible-gaming core of the advertising code.

### Obligations

| Rule | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **No targeting at-risk/problem gamblers** | Must not target people identified as at-risk or experiencing gambling harm | 256.06(1) |
| **Helpline in ALL ads** | Must include a link to and phone number for the Massachusetts Problem Gambling Helpline **using language provided by the Department of Public Health** (or other RG information required by the Commission) | 256.06(2) |
| **Unsubscribe mechanism** | Must offer an unsubscribe option in direct marketing | 256.06(5) |
| **No ads to self-excluded** | Must not advertise to self-excluded individuals | 256.07 |
| **No saturation advertising** | No saturation advertising at sporting events | 256.08(2) |

> **DPH-provided asset — confirm current creative**: 256.06(2) requires the **DPH-provided** helpline language; operators must obtain the current asset directly from the MGC/DPH. With the **PlayWell** rebrand (from GameSense, 2026-03-27), the legacy GameSense-branded creative and the `GameSenseMA.com` domain are in transition — do not reproduce the old tagline from memory.

### Responsible gaming font-size requirements

Massachusetts prescribes **specific font-size minimums** for responsible gaming messaging in sports wagering advertising (205 CMR 256.06(4)). Give all messaging visual dignity — never shrink to the minimum.

| Medium | Minimum size requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Print** | Same size as the majority of text OR 2% of the greater dimension of the ad | 256.06(4)(a) |
| **Billboard** | 5% of the greater dimension | 256.06(4)(b) |
| **Digital billboard** | Visible for the entire duration of the ad | 256.06(4)(c) |
| **Video/TV — Option A** | Visible for the entire duration of the ad, minimum 2% of screen | 256.06(4)(d) |
| **Video/TV — Option B** | 2% of screen from the first wagering reference + a dedicated end screen at 8% of screen for the last 3 seconds | 256.06(4)(d) |
| **Websites** | Same size as the majority of text on the page | 256.06(4)(e) |

## Required disclosures

Every sports wagering advertisement in Massachusetts must include:

| Disclosure | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **21+ age statement** | Must state 21+ | 256.05(1) |
| **Helpline** | Link + phone using DPH-provided language, meeting font-size minimums | 256.06(2), 256.06(4) |
| **Financial relationship disclosure** | For endorsements / paid relationships | 256.01(4) |
| **Full promotion terms** | All material conditions conspicuously specified; "free" only when accurate | 256.04(3), 256.04(5) |

## Casino advertising

Massachusetts has **no separate prescriptive casino advertising code**. Casino RG communication is **obligation-based**: licensees post responsible gambling information per their Commission-approved internal controls plan (205 CMR 138), with the statutory duty anchored in M.G.L. c. 23K § 18(6). The casino-side responsible-gaming program — **PlayWell** (formerly GameSense), including PlayWell Info Centers and **PlayMyWay** voluntary budgeting — is the primary on-floor RG vehicle.

Because casino signage has no mandated wording, operators have full latitude to use {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice, provided the obligation (clear RG information + helpline) is met. Self-excluded-patron marketing bans apply (M.G.L. c. 23K § 45; 205 CMR 133.06).

For casino-side RG tooling and on-brand copy, see [Player protection — land-based](README.md#player-protection--land-based) in the main module.

### General guidance

- Any {{PROGRAM_NAME}} template used in a sports wagering promotional context must carry the **DPH-provided** helpline language at the prescribed font size, plus 21+.
- The operator is responsible for **all** advertising on its behalf, including agency and third-party placements (256.01(1)) — brief partners on these rules.
- Educational content (game guides, odds explainers) distributed as responsible-gaming resources is not "advertising," but including the MA helpline remains best practice.
- Confirm the **current** DPH RG creative with the MGC/DPH before any sports wagering launch — the GameSense-to-PlayWell transition is in progress.
