# Young Adults

Messaging for {{MIN_AGE}}–25 players — digital-native, social-media-fluent, often new to real-money gambling. First experiences shape long-term habits, so education works best before patterns form.

> **Operator note**: This segment overlaps with [General Players](general-players.md) but has distinct channel preferences and messaging needs. Young adults are not a separate species — they're sharp, skeptical, and allergic to being talked down to. Treat them as smart adults who happen to be new to this. Replace `{{MIN_AGE}}` with your jurisdiction's minimum gambling age. See [Voice and Tone](../../brand-book/04-voice-and-tone.md) for the full framework.

## Segment profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Players aged {{MIN_AGE}}–25, often new to real-money gambling |
| **Demographics** | Digital-native, social-media-fluent, mobile-first |
| **Motivations** | Social activity, entertainment, curiosity, peer influence, sports fandom |
| **Relationship to gambling** | New or relatively new. May have experience with free-to-play games but not real-money risk. |
| **Products** | Sports betting (often first entry), slots on mobile, social casino games transitioning to real-money |

| **Peer influence** | High. Friend groups often bet together. Social proof is powerful. |
| **Self-perception** | "I know what I'm doing." (They may not, but telling them that won't help.) |

## Key insight

> **First experiences shape long-term habits. Education works best before patterns form — not after. This segment is building their gambling habits right now. The information they receive today defines their relationship with gambling for years.**

Young adults have grown up with free-to-play games, loot boxes, and social casino apps. The transition to real-money gambling feels familiar but operates on fundamentally different economics. The house edge, the vig, the math — these are new concepts even if the interface feels familiar.

The opportunity: reach them early with genuinely useful information, framed as insider knowledge rather than safety warnings. If Playbook content is the first thing they learn about gambling, they'll build better habits from the start.

---

## Message priorities

In order of importance for this segment:

1. **How odds actually work** — House edge, RNG, probability, vig. The transition from "free-to-play" to "real-money" math. Framed as knowledge that gives them an edge (over their uninformed friends, not over the house).
2. **Budget-first mindset** — Set your entertainment budget before you start. Normalize budgeting as the default, not as a response to a problem.
3. **Social proof** — Peers who use tools. Friends who know the odds. Make informed play the socially desirable position.
4. **Myth-busting** — They've absorbed myths from friends, social media, and culture. Correct early.
5. **Tool onboarding** — Set up limits as part of the onboarding experience, not as an afterthought.

## Tone guidance

**Primary archetype**: The Sharp (Playful / Witty → Confident / Informative)

Young adults respond to confidence and authenticity. They can spot corporate-speak instantly. The Sharp treats them as equals who just happen to not know this particular thing yet.

| Register | When to use with this segment |
|---|---|
| **Playful / Witty** | Social media, quiz hooks, myth-busting, peer challenges |
| **Confident / Informative** | Odds education, tool descriptions, onboarding flows |
| **Warm / Direct** | Budget setup, session reminders, check-in moments |
| **Celebratory** | Quiz scores, first limit set, achievement moments |

### Critical tone note

**Not condescending about age.** Never reference their youth as a reason they need education. "You're young, so here's what you need to know" is instant dismissal. "Here's how odds work — most people don't know this" treats them as equals in a larger group of people who haven't learned this yet.

## Sample messages

### Onboarding

> "Welcome. Quick setup: set your budget, set your pace, take the odds quiz. Under a minute. Then you're ready."

> "New to real-money play? The math is different from free-to-play. Here's the 60-second version."

> "First thing: set your entertainment budget for the week. Think of it like a subscription — you know the cost upfront."

### First deposit

> "Your first deposit. Before you start: the house always has an edge. That's how the business works. Knowing your edge makes the experience better."

> "Setting a deposit limit with your first deposit? That's a sharp move. Pick your number."

> "Your entertainment budget is yours to set. This is the cost of the experience, not an investment."

### Social media

> "Think you know how odds work? 73% of players we surveyed got question 3 wrong. Take the quiz. Link in bio."

> "Your friend's 'guaranteed system' for sports betting? It's guaranteed to do exactly one thing: lose money over time. Here's why. [link]"

> "The house edge on that slot? Somewhere between 2% and 15%. That's a wide range. Know yours before you spin."

> "A 4-leg parlay: 6.25% chance of hitting. The payout looks big because the odds are tiny. Share this with your group chat."

### Quiz hooks

> "How well do you actually know the odds? 10 questions. 2 minutes. Challenge your friends."

> "Your friend thinks they know more about odds than you. There's one way to settle this. Take the quiz."

> "Only 23% of players got this question right. Think you can? [link]"

### Session reminder

> "You've been playing for {{DURATION}}. Quick check-in. Your activity dashboard has the numbers."

> "{{DURATION}} session so far. Most players your level average about 30 minutes. Just a heads-up."

### Tool promotion

> "Deposit limits: set it once, play without thinking about it. Like setting a budget for any other entertainment."

> "Players who set up their tools during onboarding report better experiences. It takes 30 seconds."

## Channel emphasis

| Channel | Priority | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Social media (Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X)** | Primary | This is where they live. Content must be native to these platforms. |
| **Push notifications** | Primary | Mobile-first audience. Push notifications reach them directly. |
| **In-app** | Primary | Messages within the gambling app itself. Contextual and timely. |
| **YouTube / video** | Secondary | Explainer videos, quiz content, odds education. |
| **Discord / Twitch** | Secondary | Community-based engagement, especially for sports bettors. |
| **Email** | Low | Young adults check email less frequently. Use only for account-critical communications. |
| **Blog / long-form** | Low | Most won't seek out long-form content. Use video or social instead. |

### Platform-native content tips

- **Instagram/TikTok**: Short, visual, shareable. Carousel posts for myth-busting. Reels for quick odds explainers.
- **Twitter/X**: One-line facts, quiz hooks, challenge prompts. Thread format for deeper content.
- **Push notifications**: Under 50 characters. One idea. One action.
- **In-app**: Contextual to what they're doing. Deposit screen → budget message. Session timeout → reminder.

## What to avoid

| Don't | Why | Do instead |
|---|---|---|
| Reference their age as a reason for education | "You're young, so you need to learn this" is condescending and dismissive. | "Most people don't know this" — frames them in the larger group, not as a special case. |
| Use parental tone | "We want to make sure you're safe" sounds like a parent. They'll disengage. | "Here's how it works. Now you know." Peer-level, not parent-to-child. |
| Say "you're too young to understand" | Obvious, but it happens implicitly. Any suggestion that they can't handle information is an insult. | Give them the full information. Trust them with it. |
| Use age-based stereotypes | "Millennials and Gen Z need help with..." — categorizing by generation is reductive. | Address them as individuals with specific interests and knowledge gaps. |
| Send email as primary channel | They'll miss it or ignore it. | Use social media, push notifications, and in-app messaging. |
| Assume they've never gambled | Many have experience with social casino apps, free-to-play games, or informal betting. | "Whether you're new to real-money play or just new to the math behind it..." |
| Use long-form content as primary format | They'll scroll past it. | Short, visual, platform-native content. Link to deeper content for those who want it. |
| Compare to older players | "Unlike experienced players, you need..." creates an unwanted hierarchy. | Treat all players as peers. Age is irrelevant to the quality of information. |
