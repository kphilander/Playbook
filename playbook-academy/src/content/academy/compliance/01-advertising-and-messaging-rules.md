## Section 1: Mandatory Messaging Requirements

### Reading

In Module 00, you learned about obligation types: verbatim, obligation-based, and standard-based. This section puts them into practice across the messaging requirements you'll encounter in jurisdiction modules.

**Verbatim requirements** leave no room for modification. When a regulator prescribes exact wording, that wording must appear character-for-character. For example, Nevada's Regulation 5.225(18)(b) prescribes a specific message for wagering account access. The prescribed text must appear exactly as mandated.

The {{PROGRAM_NAME}} approach to verbatim requirements: **surround, don't substitute.** Place the mandated text in full, then add {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice around it. The required words appear exactly as specified; the surrounding copy provides context, warmth, and a path to action.

**Obligation-based requirements** specify what must be communicated without dictating the words. Nevada's Regulation 5.170 for general signage is a classic example: post problem gambling information and the helpline number in conspicuous places — but the phrasing is up to the operator. This is where {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice has full flexibility.

Here's the difference in practice:

| Approach | General signage (obligation-based) |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| **Bare compliance** | "If you or someone you know has a gambling problem, call 1-800-GAMBLER." |
| **{{PROGRAM_NAME}} on-brand** | "Free, confidential support — 24/7. For any question about gambling. **Call 1-800-GAMBLER** &#124; **Chat at ncpgambling.org**" |

Both meet the regulatory obligation. The second version provides multiple contact options, frames the helpline as available for any question (not only crisis), and matches the design quality of the surrounding commercial content.

**Standard-based requirements** fall in between. Specific items must appear (helpline name, program name, age restriction) but the surrounding copy is flexible. The rule: display required items exactly as specified, use {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice for everything else.

**Rules for on-brand integration of mandatory messaging** (from the jurisdiction module template):
1. Verbatim statements appear word-for-word — never paraphrase legally required language
2. Obligation-based requirements can use full {{PROGRAM_NAME}} voice as long as the obligation is met
3. Give all messaging visual dignity — don't shrink to minimum size
4. Follow with a helpful action whenever possible (link to tools, chat option, additional resources)

## Section 2: Advertising Restrictions

### Reading

Advertising is where regulatory requirements and brand voice collide most often. Jurisdiction modules include an advertising restrictions section that covers channel-specific rules, prohibited content, and required disclosures. Here's what to look for:

**Common advertising restrictions across jurisdictions:**

| Category | Common requirements |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Truthfulness** | Advertising must not be false, misleading, or deceptive. Must not misrepresent odds of winning. |
| **Vulnerable audiences** | Must not target minors or people who have self-excluded. Many jurisdictions restrict advertising near schools, playgrounds, or youth-oriented media. |
| **Inducement limits** | Restrictions on bonus offers, free bets, and promotional incentives. Some jurisdictions cap the value or require disclosure of wagering requirements. |
| **Required disclosures** | Helpline number, age restriction, and/or mandatory messaging must appear in all advertising. |
| **Channel-specific rules** | Broadcast (TV/radio), digital, print, direct marketing, in-venue, and sponsorship may each have different restrictions. |

**What {{PROGRAM_NAME}} advertising content should never do:**
- Suggest gambling is a way to solve financial problems
- Imply that skill can overcome house edge in games of chance
- Target minors or use imagery/language that appeals primarily to underage audiences
- Pressure players to gamble ("don't miss out," "last chance," "you'd be crazy not to")
- Minimize the cost of gambling or obscure the terms of promotions

**What {{PROGRAM_NAME}} advertising content should always include:**
- Helpline number or support link — integrated into the design, not buried as fine print
- Age restriction notice appropriate to the jurisdiction
- Any jurisdiction-specific required disclosures

The jurisdiction module's advertising restrictions section provides a detailed reference for each market. Before creating or approving any advertising content, cross-reference it against the relevant module. When in doubt about a specific claim or approach, consult legal/compliance before publication.

## Section 3: Co-Branding and Placement

### Reading

The [Application Guidelines]({{PLAYBOOK_REPO}}/blob/main/brand-book/07-application-guidelines.md) define how {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content appears alongside an operator's commercial brand. The core principle: **integration over isolation.** {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content should be woven into the player experience, not siloed into a separate page that nobody visits.

**Co-branding visual hierarchy rules:**

| Rule | Rationale |
|------|-----------|
| {{PROGRAM_NAME}} logo no smaller than 60% of operator logo height | Prevents visual subordination |
| Same or larger font size as surrounding commercial copy | Prevents the "fine print" effect |
| Helpline numbers in bold or distinct weight | Ensures readability |
| Adequate white space around {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content | Prevents visual crowding |

**The integration test** (from Chapter 7): If a player can use your platform for a full session without encountering any {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content unless they actively go looking for it, the integration has failed.

**{{PROGRAM_NAME}} content placement requirements:**

| Touchpoint | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| **Every page** | Footer strip with logomark, helpline, age restriction, and content hub link |
| **Registration / onboarding** | Introduction to tools, optional limit-setting |
| **First deposit** | Deposit limit prompt — optional but prominent |
| **During play** | Session awareness at configurable frequency |
| **Account settings** | Full dashboard — limits, reminders, activity, quizzes |
| **Email** | Helpline in every email footer; same font size as commercial content |
| **Social media** | Every profile bio includes helpline or support link |

**When {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content appears alongside commercial advertising:**
- {{PROGRAM_NAME}} elements complement the promotion, not contradict it
- Helpline information is integrated, not tacked on as fine print
- The transition between commercial and {{PROGRAM_NAME}} messaging feels natural
- {{PROGRAM_NAME}} messaging uses the same production quality as the commercial content

The goal: a player seeing a promotional ad with {{PROGRAM_NAME}} messaging should think "this brand takes me seriously" — not "here's the legal disclaimer."

## Key Takeaways

> **Narrator:** [transition slide] Let's recap what you've learned.

- **Verbatim means verbatim** — prescribed text appears word-for-word; use brand voice in the surrounding copy only

- **Integration over isolation** — {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content is woven into the player experience, not bolted on as an afterthought
