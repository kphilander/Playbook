# Player Tools Walkthrough

In All Employees, you learned that the Knowledge Gap is the distance between what players know and what they need to know. These six tools directly close that gap — they give players visibility into their own play and control over their spending, time, and access.

This module teaches you what each tool does, how it works under the hood, and how to explain it to a player in plain language. You're not selling these tools — you're making sure players know they exist and understand how to use them.

## Learning objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

1. Name all six player-facing tools and describe what each one does in one sentence
2. Explain the mechanics, timing, and reversibility of each tool to a player
3. Distinguish between a cool-off period and self-exclusion by duration, reversibility, and framing
4. Guide a player to the right tool based on what they're asking for

## Section 1: The Tool Kit

### Reading

Players have access to six tools in their account settings under the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} section. Every frontline staff member needs to know what each one does and how to describe it without jargon or judgment.

Here is the full set:

| Tool | What it does | One-sentence explanation for players |
|---|---|---|
| **Deposit limits** | Caps how much a player can deposit in a day, week, or month | "You pick your amount and your timeframe — daily, weekly, or monthly — and the system won't let you deposit more than that." |
| **Session reminders** | Sends a check-in notification at an interval the player chooses | "You set the timer — say, every 30 minutes or every hour — and you'll get a heads-up showing how long you've been playing." |
| **Activity dashboard** | Shows play stats — sessions, time, spending patterns | "It's your activity summary — total sessions, play time, spending patterns, all in one place." |
| **Bankroll planner** | Calculates estimated play time based on budget and game type | "Put in your budget and the game you want to play, and it'll show you roughly how long your money could last." |
| **Cool-off period** | Pauses the account for 24 hours, 7 days, or 30 days | "It pauses your account for the time you choose. When the period ends, it reactivates automatically." |
| **Self-exclusion** | Closes the account for 6 months, 1 year, 5 years, or permanently | "It closes your account for a longer period. During that time, you can't log in and you won't receive any marketing." |

There's also the **Game IQ quiz** in the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content hub — a 7-question odds quiz that takes about 2 minutes. It's not an account tool, but it's worth mentioning when players are curious about how games work.

**Where players find their tools:** Account settings > {{PROGRAM_NAME}} section. All tools are in one place. The content hub (quizzes, articles, odds information) is accessible from primary navigation at {{CONTENT_HUB_URL}}.

## Section 2: Mechanics That Matter

### Reading

Knowing what each tool does is the first step. Knowing how each tool behaves — timing, reversibility, and what happens at the boundaries — is what makes you useful to a player who has questions.

**Deposit limits — the cooling-off principle:**

Setting or reducing a deposit limit takes effect **immediately**. But increasing or removing a limit has a cooling-off period of {{COOL_OFF_PERIOD}} before it takes effect. This is by design — it protects the player's original decision. You cannot override this. No staff member can override this. It applies to everyone.

When a player hits their limit, they see a notification telling them they've reached it and when it resets. The message is supportive, not punitive: "That's you playing on your own terms."

**Session reminders — the player controls the interval:**

The player sets the interval (e.g., every 30 minutes, every hour). During play, a reminder appears showing time played and activity so far. The player can dismiss it and keep playing, or take a break. It's information, not a lock-out.

**Activity dashboard — neutral framing:**

The dashboard shows total sessions, total play time, average session length, net result, deposit history, and limit usage. All data is player-facing and designed to be informative, not alarming. It uses "activity summary" — never "loss report."

**Cool-off vs. self-exclusion — know the difference:**

| | Cool-off | Self-exclusion |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 24 hours / 7 days / 30 days | 6 months / 1 year / 5 years / permanent |
| **Reversible?** | Account reactivates automatically when the period ends | Cannot be reversed during the exclusion period |
| **Marketing** | Paused during cool-off | Permanently removed during exclusion |
| **Your language** | "Take a break" / "Pause your account" | "Self-exclusion" (use in Tier 2 / formal contexts only) |
| **Player's balance** | Withdrawable during cool-off | Returned via standard withdrawal method |

**The fundamental rule:** Player-set limits and exclusions cannot be overridden by staff. This is not a suggestion — it is a design principle. The player's decision stands.

> **Operator integration point**: Your organization's self-exclusion process may include additional steps beyond what's described here (e.g., multi-channel exclusion, third-party registry enrollment). Link your complete process documentation here: [Self-Exclusion Process]({{SELF_EXCLUSION_PROCESS_URL}})

> **Operator integration point**: Your product's specific tool UI, navigation paths, and any additional features beyond the standard set are covered in your product training. Link here: [Product Training]({{PRODUCT_TRAINING_URL}})

## Section 3: Guiding Players to the Right Tool

### Reading

Players don't always know the name of the tool they need. They describe what they want, and your job is to translate that into the right feature. Here's how common player requests map to tools:

| When a player says... | They probably need... | Your response starts with... |
|---|---|---|
| "I want to set a budget" / "How do I limit my spending?" | Deposit limits | "Great question. You can set a deposit limit — daily, weekly, or monthly — right in your account settings." |
| "How long have I been playing?" / "Can I get a reminder?" | Session reminders | "You can set a session reminder at any interval you choose. It'll give you a heads-up during play." |
| "I want to see my play history" / "Where can I check my stats?" | Activity dashboard | "Your activity dashboard shows all of that — sessions, time, spending patterns. It's in your account settings." |
| "How far will my money go?" / "What should I budget for tonight?" | Bankroll planner | "The bankroll planner can help with that. Put in your budget and the game you want to play." |
| "I need a break" / "I want to stop for a while" | Cool-off (short) or self-exclusion (long) | "Absolutely. Can I ask — are you looking for a short break, like a few days, or something longer?" |
| "How do the odds work?" / "What are my chances?" | Game IQ quiz / content hub | "We have a full odds breakdown in our {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content hub. There's also a quick quiz if you want to test your knowledge." |

**The key principle:** Don't prescribe. Offer options and let the player choose. "You can" is always better than "you should." When a player asks about taking a break, start by asking what kind of break they're looking for — don't jump to the most restrictive option.

**When a player asks for something beyond your scope** — like financial advice, counseling, or an override of their self-set limits — you don't have to solve it. Acknowledge their question and point them to the right resource. "I can't advise on that, but the helpline at {{HELPLINE_NUMBER}} can help. They're free, confidential, and available 24/7."

### Narrator Script

> **Narrator:** [transition slide] You know the tools. You know the mechanics. Now let's talk about the moment that matters — when a player asks you about them.
>
> [show mapping table] Players don't walk up and say "I'd like to configure my deposit limit parameters." They say "I want to set a budget" or "How do I limit my spending?" Your job is to translate what they're saying into the right tool and walk them there.
>
> [highlight break request] The trickiest one is when someone says "I need a break." That could mean a 24-hour cool-off or a six-month self-exclusion. Don't guess. Ask: "Are you looking for a short break or something longer?" Let them tell you.
>
> [pause] And remember — you're not a counselor. If a player asks for financial advice or wants you to override a limit they set, you don't have to solve it. Acknowledge, and point them to the right resource.
>

## Key Takeaways

> **Narrator:** [transition slide] Let's recap.

- **Six tools**: deposit limits, session reminders, activity dashboard, bankroll planner, cool-off, self-exclusion. Know them all, explain each in one sentence.
- **The cooling-off principle**: reducing a limit is instant; increasing or removing a limit has a waiting period. You cannot override it. It protects the player's choice.
- **Cool-off vs. self-exclusion**: cool-off is short (up to 30 days, automatic reactivation). Self-exclusion is long (6 months to permanent, irreversible during the period).
- **Don't prescribe**: offer options and let the player choose. "You can" — never "you should."
- **When in doubt**: point the player to the {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content hub, the helpline, or your supervisor. You don't have to solve everything.
