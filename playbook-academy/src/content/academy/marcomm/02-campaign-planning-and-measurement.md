## Section 4: Measurement

### Reading

The [player testing protocol]({{PLAYBOOK_REPO}}/blob/main/docs/player-testing-protocol.md) defines how to measure whether {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content actually works. It includes two survey types:

**Pulse Survey (3 items):** Quick check after any content exposure.
- Brand recognition: "Have you seen {{PROGRAM_NAME}} content before?"
- Recall: "What do you remember about it?"
- Net Promoter: "How likely are you to recommend this content?"

**Full Survey (7 items):** Deeper assessment for campaign evaluation.
- Adds: comprehension, behavior change, trust impact, and overall satisfaction
- Uses semantic differentials and Likert scales

**What to measure:**

| Metric | What it tells you | Where to find it |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Content engagement** | Are players interacting? | Quiz completions, time on page, share rate |
| **Tool adoption** | Are players using features? | Deposit limit set rate, session reminder usage |
| **Brand recall** | Do players remember the content? | Pulse survey, delayed recall test |
| **Comprehension** | Do players understand the concepts? | Quiz scores, full survey |
| **Behavioral impact** | Are players changing how they play? | Tool usage trends, session patterns |

**What NOT to measure:** Don't try to measure "problem gambling prevention" or "harm reduction" rates. These are clinical outcomes that require longitudinal research and clinical methodology — not marketing metrics. {{PROGRAM_NAME}} measures content effectiveness, not clinical outcomes.

### A/B Testing for Campaign Optimization

A/B testing lets you compare two versions of the same deliverable to find what works better. Use it when you need evidence, not intuition.

**When to A/B test:**
- Comparing cultural adaptations (default profile vs. localized version)
- Testing message variants (different hooks for the same concept)
- Evaluating different CTAs (action-oriented vs. curiosity-driven)
- Testing channel effectiveness (email vs. in-app for the same message)

**Test design:** Split your audience into two groups. Group A (control) sees the default version. Group B (variant) sees the modified version. Same audience composition, same timing, one different variable. If you change more than one thing, you can't isolate what caused the difference.

**Sample size:** Minimum 30 per group (60 total) for meaningful results. For subtle differences — like a slightly reworded CTA — aim higher (100+ per group). The smaller the expected difference, the larger the sample you need.

**What to measure:** Use the player testing protocol's semantic differential pairs. The two most diagnostic pairs for A/B testing are:
- **"Generic / Made for me"** (1–7 scale) — tests whether the variant feels more personalized
- **"Boring / Engaging"** (1–7 scale) — tests whether the variant captures attention better

**Significance:** A difference of 0.5 points or more on the 7-point scale is a meaningful improvement. Below 0.5, the difference may be real but is unlikely to justify the cost of switching versions.

## Section 5: From Brief to Launch

### Reading

A campaign strategy is only as good as the process that moves it from draft to deployment. This section covers the approval workflow — the steps between finishing a brief and launching it live.

**The approval workflow:**

Every campaign follows this sequence: Draft → Peer review (brand rubric) → Brand owner sign-off → Compliance review (jurisdiction check) → Production handoff → QA → Launch. No step is optional. No campaign ships without clearing each gate.

**What a production handoff includes:**
- **Copy deck** — all messages with their IDs from the message library
- **Template references** — specific templates from `collateral/render/` mapped to each deliverable
- **Placeholder values** — all `{{PLACEHOLDER}}` tokens resolved with operator-specific values
- **Cultural profile** — confirmed adaptation settings (voice, framing, humor, directness, comfort)
- **Channel specs** — dimensions, formats, character limits, and delivery requirements for each channel

**Sample timeline:** For a standard campaign, expect approximately 2–3 weeks from brief to launch. Complex campaigns with multiple jurisdictions or custom assets may take longer. The sequence matters more than the exact duration — rushing a gate creates downstream problems.

**Sign-off checklist:**

| Step | Owner | What they check |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Peer review | Another content creator | Voice principles, stigma-free language, specificity |
| Brand owner | Program lead | Pillar alignment, tone register, overall quality |
| Compliance | Legal/regulatory | Jurisdiction requirements, mandatory messaging, advertising rules |
| Production | Designer/developer | Visual identity specs, accessibility, template rendering |
| QA | Brand owner + compliance | Final check before deployment |

> **Operator integration point**: Your product-specific deployment guidance lives at [Product Training]({{PRODUCT_TRAINING_URL}}). Map your internal approval process to these gates, adding any org-specific steps (e.g., executive review, localization QA) as needed.

### Narrator Script

> **Narrator:** [transition slide] You've built the strategy, written the copy, and planned the measurement. Now let's talk about the last mile — how a brief becomes a launched campaign.
>
> [show workflow] Every campaign follows the same path: draft, peer review, brand owner sign-off, compliance review, production handoff, QA, and launch. Seven gates.
>
> [key point] Peer review against the brand rubric is not optional. This is where another creator checks your work against the voice principles, stigma-free language standards, and specificity requirements you learned in Module 1. It catches problems that the writer is too close to see.
>
> [show sign-off table] Each gate has a different owner checking different things. The peer reviewer checks the copy craft. The brand owner checks strategic alignment. Compliance checks the jurisdiction rules. Production checks the visual specs. And QA is the final pass before anything goes live.
>
> [show production handoff] The production handoff is where strategy becomes deliverables. It includes the full copy deck with message IDs, template references, resolved placeholder values, the confirmed cultural profile, and channel specs. Everything the designer or developer needs to build — nothing left ambiguous.
>
> [transition] The system is designed so that the right eyes see the work at the right time. No campaign ships without this process.
