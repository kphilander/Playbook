## Section 1: The Content Scorecard

### Reading

You A/B test email subject lines. You measure campaign NPS. Your Playbook material should get the same treatment -- because Tier 1 material is a product, not a pamphlet.

**The philosophy:** Playbook material competes for the same player attention as your sportsbook promos, casino loyalty emails, and brand campaigns. If you'd measure those with NPS, measure this with NPS. The quality bar is commercial-grade, not "good enough for compliance."

### NPS: the anchor metric

One question: **"How likely would you recommend this material to a friend who gambles?"** Scored 0-10, calculated as % Promoters (9-10) minus % Detractors (0-6). The same metric you'd use for your app, your loyalty program, or your sportsbook UX.

| NPS | Signal | What it means |
|-----|--------|--------------|
| **50+** | Exceptional | Players are sharing this material organically -- on par with Netflix/Spotify (50-70 NPS) |
| **30-49** | Strong | Matches gaming/sportsbook app benchmarks (30-50 NPS). Ship confidently. |
| **0-29** | Improve | Positive but below target. Run diagnostics with the Full survey. |
| **Below 0** | Problem | More detractors than promoters. Revise before shipping. |

> Traditional compliance material is unmeasured -- nobody even asks players what they think. Playbook targets the same NPS range as the products it sits alongside. That's the difference between a pamphlet and a product.

### The five semantic pairs

NPS tells you *whether* material is working. The semantic pairs tell you *why* -- or why not. Each pair is a 1-7 scale measuring a different dimension of quality:

1. **Forgettable -- Memorable:** Message stickiness. Will players remember this tomorrow?
2. **Boring -- Engaging:** Entertainment value. Did it hold attention or feel like homework?
3. **Preachy -- Respectful:** Voice register. This is the critical pair -- it catches material that lectures. **(Brand DNA Alarm trigger)**
4. **Confusing -- Clear:** Comprehension. Can players understand the message on first read?
5. **Generic -- Made for me:** Cultural resonance. Does it feel targeted or one-size-fits-all?

These pairs are grounded in validated research methodology: semantic differential scales (Osgood et al., 1957), NPS (Reichheld, 2003), and perceived message effectiveness (Dillard et al., 2007). You don't need a research background to use them -- players score each pair and the numbers do the talking.

### Scoring thresholds

| Average score | Signal | Action |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| **5.5+** | Strong | Ship -- this dimension is working |
| **4.0-5.4** | Acceptable | Improve if possible, but not blocking |
| **Below 4.0** | Investigate | Check open-ended feedback for this dimension |
| **Below 3.0** | Red flag | Material is failing on this dimension -- revise |

**Brand DNA Alarm:** If Preachy-Respectful scores below 4.0, stop. Your material may be lecturing players -- the single biggest failure mode for literacy material. This pair can override an otherwise strong scorecard. Revise against the voice principles from Module 3 before shipping.

## Section 2: Running a Scorecard

### Reading

Three surveys. One decision tree. The entire process takes 3-4 hours spread across two weeks.

### The three instruments

**Pulse** -- 3 items, under 1 min, 30 responses
- NPS (0-10)
- Forgettable-Memorable (1-7)
- Open-ended: one thing you'd change

Your default -- send after every launch.

**Full** -- 7 items, ~2 min, 30 responses (60 for A/B)
- NPS (0-10)
- All five semantic pairs (1-7)
- Open-ended feedback

Your diagnostic -- when Pulse flags issues.

**Recall** -- 4 items, 7-14 days later, 25 responses
- Free recall: "What do you remember?"
- True/false knowledge checks
- Behavioral follow-through checklist
- "Did you do anything differently?"

Your retention check -- did it actually stick?

> **Sample sizes matter.** Target 30 responses for reliable Pulse and Full results. If you're A/B testing two versions of the same material, you need 60 (30 per variant). The Recall survey can work with 25 because you're looking at open-ended patterns, not precise averages.

### The decision tree

After every launch, follow this flow:

**Step 1:** Send the Pulse survey. Three questions, under a minute, target 30 responses. This is your first read on any piece of material.

**Step 2a:** NPS 30+ and all pairs 4.0+ -> **Ship.** Material is performing. Schedule the Recall survey 7-14 days later to confirm it sticks.

**Step 2b:** NPS 0-29 or any pair below 4.0 -> **Run Full.** Something's off. The Full survey with all five pairs will pinpoint which dimension needs work.

**Step 2c:** NPS below 0 or Preachy-Respectful below 3.0 -> **Revise.** Stop shipping. Revise against Module 3 voice principles and retest.

### Worked example

Your team just launched a myth-busting campaign about slots RNG. Here are the Pulse results from 35 responses:

| Metric | Score | Signal |
|--------|-------|--------|
| NPS | +38 | Strong -- above 30 threshold |
| Forgettable-Memorable | 5.8 | Strong -- above 5.5 |

**Reading:** NPS 38 is in the Strong range. Memorable at 5.8 is also strong. This material ships. Schedule the Recall survey in 10 days to confirm retention.

Now imagine the Pulse came back differently:

| Metric | Score | Signal |
|--------|-------|--------|
| NPS | +14 | Below 30 -- needs investigation |
| Forgettable-Memorable | 4.2 | Acceptable -- not strong |

**Reading:** NPS 14 is positive but below the 30+ shipping threshold. Run the Full survey. The Full results show Preachy-Respectful at 3.6 and Generic-Made for me at 3.4. Diagnosis: the material is lecturing and it doesn't feel targeted. The Brand DNA alarm confirms it -- revise the voice and add segment-specific framing before shipping.

### Cadence

| When | Survey | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Every launch** | Pulse | First read -- non-negotiable |
| **When Pulse flags issues** | Full | Diagnostic -- find which dimensions need work |
| **7-14 days after launch** | Recall | Retention check for flagship material |
| **Quarterly** | Full | Drift check on highest-traffic material |

Total effort per launch: 3-4 hours spread across two weeks. One hour to set up and distribute the Pulse. One hour to collect and read results. One hour for Recall setup. One hour for the final read.

### Deploy the survey

Playbook ships a ready-to-use survey widget that auto-calculates NPS and all semantic pair scores with color-coded results. Choose whichever deployment method fits your stack:

| Method | Setup time | Best for |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Embed widget** | 5 min | Drop an iframe into any page. Auto-scoring, export-ready. Recommended. |
| **Google Forms** | 10 min | Free, familiar. Manual scoring in a spreadsheet. |
| **Typeform / SurveyMonkey** | 15 min | Polished UX, built-in analytics. Paid plans may be needed. |

> **Narrator:** Three surveys. One decision tree. The entire process takes three to four hours spread across two weeks. The Pulse is your default. ## Key Takeaways

- **Tier 1 material is a product, not a pamphlet** -- measure it with the same rigor as your entertainment content
- **NPS is the anchor metric:** "How likely would you recommend this material to a friend who gambles?" -- target 30+ to ship
- **Five semantic pairs** diagnose *why* material works or doesn't: Forgettable-Memorable, Boring-Engaging, Preachy-Respectful, Confusing-Clear, Generic-Made for me
- **Brand DNA Alarm:** Preachy-Respectful below 4.0 is an automatic hold -- regardless of other scores
- **Three instruments:** Pulse (every launch), Full (diagnostics), Recall (7-14 day retention check)
- **Decision tree:** NPS 30+ and all pairs 4.0+ = ship; NPS 0-29 or any pair below 4.0 = run Full; NPS below 0 or Preachy-Respectful below 3.0 = revise
- **Total effort:** 3-4 hours across two weeks per launch -- less time than a single campaign brief
