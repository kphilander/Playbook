People in Visuals
No illustrated people. Use photography.

Illustrations are for data, concepts, and diagrams — never for depicting people. When a visual needs to show a person or group, use photography following the photography guidelines.

Illustration
Charts, odds, probability, house edge, RNG, myth vs fact, quiz cards, stat graphics
Photography
Friends, fans, social moments, casino environments, anyone using a device, any human presence

Do / Don't
Do

Brand colors, bold value labels, minimal axis chrome. The data speaks — not the decoration.

Don't

Use non-brand colors, skip axes and labels, use sharp corners, or overcrowd with too many data points.

Do

Keep one concept per illustration. Simple, focused, with the key insight obvious within 2 seconds.

Don't

Overcomplicate with multiple concepts, thin inconsistent strokes, or decorative elements that don't convey meaning.
